# Repentance, from the Vinaya's Disclosure to the Repentance of Non-Arising
## 《懺——從律藏發露到無生之懺》

**Volume 3 of [The Six Practice Gates](../README.md)** — 懺 KṢAMĀ, the one subtractive gate; sequence position three, between 施 (giving) and 戒 (restraint).

*KṢAMĀ — Repentance, from the Vinaya's Disclosure to the Repentance of Non-Arising*

A companion to [*Mindful of the Buddha*](../nian/) (Volume 1) and [*The Practice of Giving*](../dana/) (Volume 2). Where DĀNA loosens the grip of "mine" (the self's death), 懺 clears karmic obstruction upon that ground of no-self, and 戒 (Volume 4) receives the new precept-body (rebirth) — 施 → 懺 → 戒 is the death-and-rebirth seam at the sequence's core.

Of the six gates, 懺 alone correlates not with a Heart Sūtra **verb** but with its **emptiness** (*śūnyatā*: 色即是空、諸法空相): the one *subtractive* gate. Its noumenal face — *lǐ-chàn* (理懺), the repentance of non-arising, the contemplation that the very nature of transgression is empty — carries this correlation; its phenomenal face — *shì-chàn* (事懺), disclosure and the Saṅgha's purification machinery — is the floor on which the volume opens. The two faces are held distinct throughout.

### Planned Structure

Eight parts, an ascent from phenomenal disclosure to the repentance of non-arising:

- **Part I** Pāli Roots — *kṣama* / *deśanā* / *kaukṛtya*; the Vinaya's disclosure (發露), *uposatha*, *pavāraṇā*
- **Part II** The Āgama View — remorse (悔 / 追悔), lawful disclosure, repentance as the Saṅgha's purification mechanism
- **Part III** The Seven-Limb Offering and Samantabhadra's Confession of Karmic Obstruction
- **Part IV** The Death-and-Rebirth Bridge: Giving → Repentance → Restraint (施 → 懺 → 戒)
- **Part V** The Three Repentances — procedural (作法), sign-taking (取相), non-arising (無生): the Tiantai judgment, the turn from phenomenal to noumenal
- **Part VI** The Chinese Repentance Rites — the *Lotus Samādhi* rite, the *Golden Light* repentance, and the great penance-liturgies
- **Part VII** ★ The Repentance of Non-Arising = Emptiness (無生懺＝空) — the volume's summit, where repentance meets *prajñā*
- **Part VIII** Modern Diagnosis and Daily Use — against therapeutic collapse, against equivalence with confession, against shame
