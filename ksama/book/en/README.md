# KṢAMĀ: Repentance
### From the Vinaya's Disclosure to the Repentance of Non-Arising

The English edition of **《懺——從律藏發露到無生之懺》** by 釋慧鏡 (Shi Huijing) — Volume 3 of the **Six Practice Gates** (六行門).

> Repentance (*kṣama*) was never confession-and-pardon, and never the management of guilt. It begins in the Vinaya's plainest act — laying what was hidden out into the light, before others — and walks all the way to the repentance of non-arising: the seeing that the self that erred, and the transgression it committed, never had a thing in them to grasp. Among the six gates it is the one subtraction.

This is the accessible *book* layer, rendered into English from the Chinese edition and term-anchored to the bilingual scholarly corpus in this repository (`ksama/papers/`, P01–P20). It lives here at `ksama/book/en/`, a part-for-part parallel of the Chinese book under `ksama/book/`.

## The road

Eight parts, one road:

**Pāli Roots · Remorse in the Āgamas · The Sevenfold Worship and Samantabhadra · The Death-and-Rebirth Bridge · The Three Repentances · The Chinese Repentance Liturgies · The Repentance of Non-Arising · The Modern Diagnosis, and Daily Use**

What was hidden, brought into the light by stages: from the disclosure formula of the Vinaya and the day-for-day price of concealment, through Ajātaśatru's deepest remorse, Samantabhadra's confession that runs moment after moment without weariness, the death-and-rebirth bridge between the giving gate and the precept gate, the ladder of the three repentances, the ritual arenas of the Chinese liturgies, to Vimalakīrti's rebuke and the sun of wisdom that melts the frost and dew — and back, at the end, down the mountain, into daily use.

## How this edition is made

Each chapter is translated from the approved Chinese chapter and term-anchored to its source paper, so that every doctrinal term uses the rendering the author has already settled. Sūtra and śāstra quotations reuse the verified English from the papers wherever they exist. Romanizations and quick definitions are gathered in [Appendix C · Glossary](appendix/C_glossary.md); each chapter's scriptural sources are indexed in [Appendix A · Academic Notes Index](appendix/A_academic-notes-index.md).
