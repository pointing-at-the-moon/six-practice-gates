# DĀNA: The Practice of Giving
### From Pāli Roots to the Dharmadhātu

The English edition of **《施——從巴利之根到法界供養》** by 釋慧鏡 (Shi Huijing) — Volume 2 of the **Six Practice Gates** (六行門).

> Giving (*dāna*) was never a transaction of merit. It is the long work of loosening, inch by inch, the hand that grips "mine" — a road that runs from the plainest letting-go (*cāga*) the Buddha taught His householders, all the way to the dharma-offering of the Flower Ornament's whole dharma-realm.

This is the accessible *book* layer, rendered into English from the Chinese edition and term-anchored to the bilingual scholarly corpus in this repository (`dana/papers/`, P01–P31). It lives here at `dana/book/en/`, a part-for-part parallel of the Chinese book under `dana/book/`.

## The road

Nine parts, one road:

**Pāli Roots · Giving in the Āgamas · The Field of Merit in the Vinaya · From Giving to Pāramitā · The Three Wheels Empty · The Reverse Side of the Śūraṅgama · The Lotus · The Huayan Dharma-Realm · The Modern Diagnosis, and a Coda**

One hand, opening by stages: from the last morsel shared in the *Itivuttaka*, through the gold-covered ground of the Jeta Grove, the Diamond Sūtra's giving without abiding in marks, the Śūraṅgama's not-taking, the Lotus's offering of body and Dharma, to Samantabhadra's vast cultivation of offerings — and back, at the end, to the one tap on a phone, and the loosening behind it.

## How this edition is made

Each chapter is translated from the approved Chinese chapter and term-anchored to its source paper, so that every doctrinal term uses the rendering the author has already settled. Sūtra and śāstra quotations reuse the verified English from the papers wherever they exist. Romanizations and quick definitions are gathered in [Appendix C · Glossary](appendix/C_glossary.md); each chapter's scriptural sources are indexed in [Appendix A · Academic Notes Index](appendix/A_academic-notes-index.md).
