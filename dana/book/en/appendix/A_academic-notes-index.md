# Appendix A · Academic Notes Index

The main text was written to be spoken to you. So the catalog numbers, the fascicle references, the source locations—all of these have been ushered out of its pages and gathered here.

This index serves two purposes. First, it lets the reader who wants to look closer go from any chapter of the book back to the exact place in the sūtras and treatises on which that chapter rests. Second, it gives a single overview of the principal scriptures and treatises cited across the book's thirty-one chapters. Nothing in this book is invented—every chapter's argument presses down on the original wording of the texts listed below. All quotations have been collated against the CBETA Electronic Tripiṭaka (the Taishō canon and the Manji Zokuzōkyō) and the Pali Text Society (PTS) / Mahāsaṅgīti editions of the Pāli canon.

Numbering conventions: **T** refers to the Taishō Shinshū Daizōkyō (volume and text number); **X** to the Manji Shinsan Zokuzōkyō; **PTS** to Pali Text Society page references; **Vism** to the Visuddhimagga. Throughout this book the Visuddhimagga is cited by paraphrase only, never quoted directly, in keeping with the terms of its source license.
