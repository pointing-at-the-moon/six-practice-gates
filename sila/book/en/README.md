# SĪLA: The Precept
### From Pāli Vinaya to the Sign-less Precepts

The English edition of **《戒——從巴利律到無相戒》** by 釋慧鏡 (Shi Huijing)—Volume 4 of the **Six Practice Gates** (六行門).

> The precept (*sīla*) is not a rope that binds you; it is a wall that guards you—not a yoke pressed onto the body, but an ability grown into the body. The rule enters the heart and becomes its substance; even the mark "I am keeping the precept" can be set down—and all along, from the Pāli Vinaya to the sign-less precept, it is the ground on which concentration stands.

This is the accessible *book* layer, rendered into English from the Chinese edition and term-anchored to the bilingual scholarly corpus in this repository (`sila/papers/`, P01–P30). It lives here at `sila/book/en/`, a part-for-part parallel of the Chinese book under `sila/book/`.

## The road

Nine parts, one road:

**Pāli Roots · The Precept in the Āgamas · Three Vinaya Lineages · The Mahāyāna Turn · The Precept-Substance · Bhaiṣajyaguru's Twelve Vows · Prajñā's Sign-less Precept · The Complete Precept · The Modern Diagnosis**

One wall, raised by stages: from Ānanda's question near evening—"World-Honored One, what is the meaning of keeping the precepts?"—through the first precept laid down case by case, the perimeter wall and the house of receiving and following, the single seed perfumed into the storehouse, the twelve vows that guard the keeper's conditions, the Diamond Sūtra's keeping that grasps no sign, the Śūraṅgama's "gathering in the mind is the precept"—and back, at the end, to the four words left at the Buddha's passing: take the precept as your teacher.

## How this edition is made

Each chapter is translated from the approved Chinese chapter and term-anchored to its source paper, so that every doctrinal term uses the rendering the author has already settled. Sūtra and śāstra quotations reuse the verified English from the papers wherever they exist. Romanizations and quick definitions are gathered in [Appendix C · Glossary](appendix/C_glossary.md); each chapter's scriptural sources are indexed in [Appendix A · Academic Notes Index](appendix/A_academic-notes-index.md).
