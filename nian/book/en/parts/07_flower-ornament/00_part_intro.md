# Part Seven · The Flower Ornament · Buddha-Recollection Opens into the Whole Dharma-Realm

The long road of buddha-recollection has come to its last and widest country — the *Flower Ornament Sūtra*.

Through the first six parts, buddha-recollection was always framed within "one Buddha, one land, one mind." In the Flower Ornament, that frame is burst open. In the Flower Ornament world, to recollect one Buddha is to recollect all Buddhas; bow to one Buddha, and the Buddhas of the ten directions receive the bow at once; within a single dust-mote appear measureless buddha-fields, layer upon layer without end, one is all. Buddha-recollection here swells into the breathing of the whole dharma-realm.

The one who leads us into this country is the great bodhisattva Samantabhadra, with his "ten great vow-kings."

Part Seven walks these ten vows one by one. Chapter one looks first at the overall design of the ten — how they stretch buddha-recollection open, from a single act into a whole way of living. Then four chapters take the vows in four groups: reverence, praise, and offerings (one through three); repentance and rejoicing (four and five); requesting the turning of the dharma-wheel and requesting the Buddhas to abide in the world (six and seven); constantly following the Buddha in study, accommodating sentient beings, and universal dedication (eight through ten).

When these ten vows have been walked, the destination this book promised at its outset — "from early Buddhist recollection to the Flower Ornament vision" — will have been reached.
