# Part Four · The Three Pure Land Sūtras · That Land, Seen Clearly

The first three parts pushed buddha-recollection, step by step, up to a doorway: the Buddha has stepped to the front of the stage, the buddha-field has taken its first shape, and recollecting and calling have been clasped into one. But that land called "Bliss" — what it is actually like, and how one gets there — was still a distant haze.

What painted that haze into a clear, exact picture was three scriptures — known together to later generations as the Three Pure Land Sūtras.

Chapter one tells of the *Sūtra of Immeasurable Life*: the forty-eight great vows that Amitābha made while he was still the bodhisattva Dharmākara — the blueprint of the land. Chapter two tells of the *Sūtra on the Contemplation of Amitāyus*: sixteen contemplations, layer by layer, by which that land is seen within the mind. Chapter three tells of the *Amitābha Sūtra*: the adornments of the Land of Bliss, and the phrase that finally gathers everything in — "holding the name."

This part is the liveliest stretch of the whole book. From here, buddha-recollection in China truly entered every household.
