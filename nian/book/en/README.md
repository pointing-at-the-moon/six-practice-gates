# Mindful of the Buddha
### From Early Buddhist Recollection to the Flower Ornament Vision

The English edition of **《念——從阿含隨念到華嚴念佛》** by 釋慧鏡 (Shi Huijing) — Volume 1 of the **Six Practice Gates** (六行門).

> Recollection (*anussati*) is not mindfulness with a religious object. It is another track — and this book walks that track from the Buddha's own teaching to the breathing of the whole dharma-realm.

This is the accessible *book* layer, rendered into English from the Chinese edition and term-anchored to the bilingual scholarly corpus in this repository (`nian/papers/`, P01–P31). It lives here at `nian/book/en/`, a part-for-part parallel of the Chinese book under `nian/book/`.

## The road

Eight parts, one road:

**Pāli Foundations · The Earliest Chinese Translations · Early Mahāyāna · The Three Pure Land Sūtras · The Great Mahāyāna Sūtras · Two Streams · The Flower Ornament · Coda**

One word — *niàn* — walked this land for more than a thousand years. This book walks it again, from the *Mahānāma Sutta* to Samantabhadra's Ten Great Vows, and places it back in the reader's hand.

## How this edition is made

Each chapter is translated from the approved Chinese chapter and term-anchored to its source paper, so that every doctrinal term uses the rendering the author has already settled. Sūtra and śāstra quotations reuse the verified English from the papers wherever they exist. Romanizations and quick definitions are gathered in [Appendix C · Glossary](appendix/C_glossary.md); each chapter's scriptural sources are indexed in [Appendix A · Academic Notes Index](appendix/A_academic-notes-index.md).
