**Dharmākara's Vow-System as a Designed Architecture — From Twenty-Four Vows to Twenty-Nine Adornments and the Dual Purity**

## Abstract

Continuing from P07's treatment of the T0361 buddha-field ontology and P08's four-step analysis of the *niàn*-*chēng* verbal coupling, this paper returns to the textual ground of the Three Pure Land Sūtras to address the forty-eight vows of T0360 as a **systematic architecture** in their own right — not a flat list of forty-eight independent promises, but a designed architecture with soteriology as its *telos*, classification as its skeleton, and an identifiable expansion trajectory as its diachronic signature. The paper argues three things. First, the deep structure of the forty-eight vows is a **container-world / sentient-world** (*bhājana-loka* / *sattva-loka*) dual-axis distribution. Vasubandhu's *Upadeśa on the Sūtra of Immeasurable Life* (T1524) gives its systematic statement through twenty-nine adornments (17 + 8 + 4); Tanluan's *Abridged Treatise on the Meaning of the Land of Peace and Bliss* (T1957) continues the dual axis with a seventeen-plus-twelve distribution and explicitly reads the vow-system and the adornment-system as two surfaces of one structural reality. Second, between T0362's twenty-four vows and T0360's forty-eight vows, four identifiable expansion moves take place — *granularization* (splitting compound vow-content into atomic conditions), *new-category addition* (especially the hearing-the-name outward-reaching cluster), *explicit access-mechanics* (the stage of definite assurance, the even-ten-recollections clause, the all-buddhas'-praise vow), and *verbal-circulation infrastructure*. Together these are not quantity-doubling but multi-dimensional architectural refinement. Third, the eighteenth vow sits in the system as the **floor** of the access architecture — minimal condition and maximum audience — not as pinnacle nor shortcut; its presence is what allows the other forty-seven vows to have beings to save. Daochuo's *Collection on the Land of Peace and Bliss* (T1958) names this "the dominant condition of the Tathāgata's great vow-power" (*adhipati-pratyaya* of Amitābha's vow-force). The paper engages Christopher Alexander's *A Pattern Language* and Herbert Simon's *The Sciences of the Artificial* as heuristic interlocutors, and delimits three non-reducible differences (*telos*, ontology, and upgrade path) as structural boundaries.

**Keywords:** forty-eight vows, T0360 *Larger Sukhāvatīvyūha*, T0362 Zhi Qian recension, T0361 twenty-four vows, Dharmākara, Lokeśvararāja, 210 billion buddha-fields, Vasubandhu, T1524 *Upadeśa on the Sūtra of Immeasurable Life*, twenty-nine adornments, five contemplation-gates, container-world, sentient-world, Tanluan, T1957 *Abridged Treatise*, two purities, Daochuo, T1958 *Anle ji*, twelve great gates, five great benefits, eighteenth vow, stage of definite assurance, even-ten-recollections, Christopher Alexander, pattern language, Herbert Simon, *Sciences of the Artificial*, designed architecture, Three Pure Land Sūtras.

## I. Introduction: When a List Begins to Unfold into an Architecture

When Western English-speaking Buddhist readers — and Chinese-speaking readers trained in English academic conventions — first encounter the forty-eight vows of Amitābha Buddha, they almost always meet them as a **list**. Doctrinal primers, textbooks, introductions to Pure Land thought, and most English translations present the same arrangement: vow one, vow two, vow three, … down to vow forty-eight, each vow given a line or two and perhaps a brief annotation. The ergonomic virtue of this presentation is obvious; a reader comes away knowing, roughly, *which vows are in there*. The cost is **structural flatness**. The forty-eight vows come to look like forty-eight parallel and independent promises, each one answering to its own small soteriological impulse in Dharmākara's mind, none of them speaking to each other, none of them organized by anything deeper than the accident of enumeration.

This flatness obscures two things that matter.

*First*, the forty-eight vows are not a one-shot product. P07 has already traced the buddha-field ontology from the *Pratyutpanna-samādhi Sūtra* (T0418, translated in Luoyang in 179 CE) to the *Sūtra on the Buddha of Measureless Pure Equal Enlightenment* (T0361, second or third century) — the shift from the already-existing buddha-fields of the face-to-face *samādhi* to a new buddha-field that the bodhisattva Dharmaratna selects and assembles out of 210 billion existing buddha-fields. T0361 has **twenty-four** vows. By the time of T0360 — traditionally ascribed to Saṅghavarman in Cao-Wei but more plausibly translated somewhere between the fourth and fifth centuries — the number has expanded to **forty-eight**. Between the two stands Zhi Qian's alternative twenty-four-vow recension, T0362. The gap is at least a century, possibly two. And the expansion from twenty-four to forty-eight is not arithmetic doubling; it is **architectural refinement** — certain older vows are split into atomic conditions, certain new categories are introduced, certain access-mechanics are made explicit. This refinement has an internal logic, and that logic can be reconstructed.

*Second*, the forty-eight vows have a **deep structure**. Vasubandhu (fourth-to-fifth century, roughly contemporary with T0360's translation into Chinese) in his *Upadeśa on the Sūtra of Immeasurable Life* (T1524) organizes the doctrinal content of Amitābha's buddha-field under twenty-nine adornments, distributed across three categories (seventeen adornments of the buddha-land, eight of the Buddha's own person, four of the bodhisattvas). Tanluan (sixth-century Northern Wei) in his *Abridged Treatise on the Meaning of the Land of Peace and Bliss* (T1957) puts Dharmākara's forty-eight vows and Vasubandhu's twenty-nine adornments side by side and says, explicitly, that the two are two framings of the same underlying reality — one at the level of vow-utterance, the other at the level of accomplished adornment. Daochuo (seventh-century early Tang) in his *Collection on the Land of Peace and Bliss* (T1958) organizes the entire Pure Land doctrinal enterprise under twelve "great gates" (*dàmén*), and in the ninth of these he extracts from the forty-eight what he calls the "five great benefits" (*wǔ fān dà yì*) — another architectural signature that East Asian scriptural reading has left on the vow-system.

Three commentators — one Indian, two Chinese — give three different organizational tools. All three, taken together, point to a single fact: **the forty-eight vows are themselves a designed architecture**. Their structure is analyzable, their expansion trajectory is reconstructible, and their internal classification is explicit once one looks for it.

The task of this paper is to lift that fact out of the commentarial literature and back onto the text of T0360 itself, through a four-layered argument in §III.

The **first** layer: the deep structure of the forty-eight vows is a **container-world / sentient-world** dual-axis distribution — a distribution that maps onto Vasubandhu's seventeen-plus-eight-plus-four = twenty-nine, onto Tanluan's seventeen-plus-twelve "two purities," and onto the implicit classification inside the T0360 vow-text itself.

The **second** layer: four identifiable expansion moves operate between T0362's twenty-four vows and T0360's forty-eight — *granularization*, *new-category addition*, *explicit access-mechanics*, and *verbal-circulation infrastructure*. These four are not quantity-doubling but multi-dimensional architectural refinement.

The **third** layer: the eighteenth vow is the **floor** of the access architecture — minimal condition, maximum audience — not its pinnacle and not a shortcut. P08 has already analyzed the *niàn*-*chēng* verbal coupling at the eighteenth vow as a **semantic hinge**; this paper supplies the architectural correlate to that verbal analysis. The eighteenth vow is the floor of the architecture, not its summit.

The **fourth** layer: §IV engages Christopher Alexander's *A Pattern Language* and Herbert Simon's *The Sciences of the Artificial* as heuristic interlocutors. The forty-eight vows, *as* a designed object, have recognizable parallels to contemporary design theory — and also three irreducible differences (*telos*, ontology, upgrade path) that mark the limits of that parallel. §V treats three popular misreadings: the vows as a flat list, the vows as an arbitrary expression of one bodhisattva's personal will, and the vows as reducible to the eighteenth. §VI opens the horizon toward P10: if T0360 gives us the architecture at the level of *vows*, T0365 (the *Contemplation Sūtra*) gives us a complementary architecture at the level of *visualization*.

## II. Scriptural Foundations

### 2.1 T0360's Forty-Eight Vows: An Overview of the Text

The vow-pronouncement of Dharmākara Bhikṣu in fascicle one of the *Sūtra of Immeasurable Life* (T0360) forms the doctrinal backbone of the entire sūtra.

The narrative frame itself already signals architecture. The passage opens with Lokeśvararāja Buddha's response to Dharmākara, first through an ocean-simile that licenses the feasibility of supreme aspiration:

> "Suppose one were to measure the great ocean with a measuring-vessel, ladle by ladle, over countless eons — one could still exhaust its depth and obtain its precious treasure. If a person arouses a resolute mind of wholehearted effort and seeks the Way without cease, they will surely attain the fruit. What vow could they fail to fulfill?"[^1]

There follows the display of 210 billion buddha-lands (whose ontological significance P07 §3.3 has already analyzed under the "selection-combination operation" — not repeated here); Dharmākara's five-kalpa meditation on those lands; and his "gathering the pure practices of all buddha-lands." Then the exemplary moment:

> "The bhikṣu addressed the Buddha: 'May you listen attentively to what I will now fully declare — my vows.'"[^2]

"May you listen … I will now fully declare" is the preparatory opening to the enumeration of the forty-eight vows. Each vow is then cast in the conditional form, "Suppose I should attain buddhahood … if it were not so, may I not take complete enlightenment" (*shè wǒ dé fó … bù qǔ zhèngjué*). This formula is inherited from T0361's "if it were not so, I would not become a buddha," and it is the paradigmatic syntax of the Amitābha vow-lineage.

After the forty-eight vows, the text passes through the phrase "having pronounced these vows, he then uttered the following verses" into an eleven-verse recapitulation ("I make these transcendent vows / I shall attain the unsurpassed Way"), and closes with "when he had finished these verses, the great earth shook in six ways, celestial flowers rained down, spontaneous music arose, and a voice from the sky proclaimed: 'He shall surely attain perfect enlightenment.'" This is the cosmological witnessing of the vow-ocean's establishment. The recapitulation is followed directly by "Dharmākara Bhikṣu made these great vows in the presence of that buddha, before the gods, demons, Brahmā, nāgas, spirits, and the eightfold assembly … the buddha-land he would establish would be vast and open, uniquely transcendent, eternally abiding, without decay or change" — leading into the long description of countless kalpas of cultivation.

**Structural observation.** The forty-eight vows in T0360 are not an abstract list of aspirations; they are a **vow-ocean embedded in a complete narrative frame** — preceded by Lokeśvararāja's authorization, the catalog of 210 billion buddha-lands, and the five-kalpa meditation; followed by the verse recapitulation, the six-fold earth-quaking, the witnessing of the eightfold assembly, and a kalpa-long trajectory toward actualization. The frame itself is part of the architecture. The vows are not an isolated promise-list; they are a doctrinal construct with a tripartite temporal structure — before, during, after — and the before-and-after are load-bearing for the meaning of the during.

### 2.2 Classifying the Forty-Eight Vows: The Container-World / Sentient-World Dual Axis

If one sorts the forty-eight vows by subject and content, a clear dual-axis distribution emerges. The table below is the classificatory base for §III.

| Category | Axis | Vow Numbers | Sub-Total |
|---|---|---|---|
| Land-conditions (no three evil destinies, no name of unwholesomeness) | Container | 1, 2, 16 | 3 |
| Land-adornments (splendor of all things, bodhi-tree, illumination of ten directions, jewel-fragrance composition) | Container | 27, 28, 31, 32 | 4 |
| Land-enjoyments (garments-by-thought, experience of bliss equal to the *āsravakṣaya* arhat) | Container | 38, 39 | 2 |
| Buddha's own qualities (radiance, lifespan) | Sentient: Buddha | 12, 13 | 2 |
| Buddha's retinue (countless *śrāvakas*) | Sentient: Buddha-land | 14 | 1 |
| Name-circulation (all buddhas praise my name) | Sentient: Buddha / circulation | 17 | 1 |
| Bodily marks of those born there (golden color, no beauty-ugliness, thirty-two marks) | Sentient: in-land | 3, 4, 21 | 3 |
| Supernormal faculties of those born there (pubbenivāsa, divine eye, divine ear, other-minds-knowledge, *ṛddhipāda*) | Sentient: in-land | 5, 6, 7, 8, 9 | 5 |
| Mental conduct of those born there (no attachment to body) | Sentient: in-land | 10 | 1 |
| Soteriological status of those born there (stage of definite assurance, immeasurable lifespan, one-lifetime-remaining) | Sentient: in-land | 11, 15, 22 | 3 |
| Bodhisattva qualities (offering-tours, ritual vessels, preaching, *nārāyaṇa* strength, eloquence, subtle discernment, seeing buddhas in the trees, hearing dharma at will) | Sentient: bodhisattva | 23, 24, 25, 26, 29, 30, 40, 46 | 8 |
| Access for beings from elsewhere (even-ten-recollections, deathbed reception, sustained-contemplation assured birth) | Sentient: access from outside | 18, 19, 20 | 3 |
| Name/light outreach to beings elsewhere (softness under the light, forbearance on hearing the name, female-to-male transformation, constant celibacy, reverence from gods and humans, intact faculties, pure-liberation-*samādhi*, birth in noble families, accomplishment of root-virtues, universally-equal-*samādhi*, non-retrogression, three dharma-forbearances) | Sentient: outreach | 33, 34, 35, 36, 37, 41, 42, 43, 44, 45, 47, 48 | 12 |

**Totals:** Container-world — 9 vows. Sentient-world — 39 vows (of which: 4 concerning the Buddha's person, 12 concerning beings born in the land, 8 concerning bodhisattvas, 15 concerning beings outside the land).

**Proportional observation.** The ratio of container-world vows (9) to sentient-world vows (39) is 1 to 4.33. This asymmetry is crucial evidence for §3.3. The vow-system of T0360 invests its resources primarily in **beings** — Buddha, resident inhabitants of the land, and outside beings — not in **environment**. This runs against the popular image of "the Pure Land" as defined by landscape splendor; T0360 itself never treats the beauty of the land as the focal point of its vows. The land's beauty is in the service of housing the soteriological operation; it is not the soteriological point.

### 2.3 The Parallel with Zhi Qian's T0362 (Twenty-Four Vows)

Zhi Qian's *Amitābha-Samyaksaṃbuddha-Sāra-Buddha-Dāna Crossing-Over Sūtra* (T0362), a Wu-dynasty translation, enumerates twenty-four vows in its first fascicle. The bodhisattva's name here is **Dharmakara** (曇摩迦), not **Dharmakarālaya** (曇摩迦留, T0361) or **Dharmākara** (法藏, T0360). The previous buddha is **Lokavartin** (樓夷亘羅, same as T0361), where T0360 has **Lokeśvararāja** (世自在王). Each of the three translation teams produced its own renderings; none were harmonized. Even when the proper names fail to stabilize, the architectural continuity remains traceable — which is itself telling. The architecture does not depend on lexical identity of proper names.

A comparison of T0362 with T0360 reveals several representative splits and additions.

*T0362's vow one splits into T0360's vows one and two.* T0362 reads: "There shall be no hells, animals, hungry ghosts, or crawling creatures." T0360 splits this into "should there be hells, hungry ghosts, or animals in my land, I will not take complete enlightenment" (vow 1) and "should any who have ended their lives in my land fall again into the three evil destinies, I will not take complete enlightenment" (vow 2). The second clause — protection-after-arrival — is new in T0360. This is not about access but about insurance for those already admitted.

*T0362's vow four splits into T0360's vows seventeen and eighteen.* T0362 combines "name-fame in the eight directions" + "praise of virtue" + "beings who hear the name rejoicing and coming to be born." T0360 separates these into vow 17 ("all buddhas praise my name" — infrastructure for name-circulation) and vow 18 ("beings of the ten directions, with sincere joyful faith … even ten recollections" — explicit access). In T0362 the circulation and the reception are fused in one vow; T0360 separates them and further specifies access through the counting unit of *nài zhì shí niàn* (even ten recollections).

*T0362's vow fifteen ("purple-magnetic gold color + thirty-two marks") splits into T0360's vows three and twenty-one.* T0360 separates body-color (true gold color) from body-marks (the thirty-two great-person marks) into two distinct vows.

Beyond granularization, T0360 adds **whole new categories**:

- **The stage of definite assurance** (vow 11: "in my land, should those among gods and humans not dwell in the stage of definite assurance and reach final cessation, I will not take complete enlightenment"). T0362 has no parallel. This imports the Abhidharmic term *sammatta-niyāma* / *samyaktva-niyata-rāśi* — the stage of stream-entry assurance — into the Amitābha vow-system for the first time, directly linking "rebirth in the land" to "confirmed bodhisattva stage."
- **The even-ten-recollections clause** (vow 18), whose status as a semantic hinge P08 §2.3 has analyzed in detail. T0362's closest analog is vow 5 ("hearing the name, rejoicing, coming to be born"), which has no explicit counting unit.
- **The all-buddhas'-praise vow** (vow 17). The subject is "all the innumerable buddhas of the ten worlds." T0362 vow 4's subject is ambiguous; T0360 separates this out into a distinct vow, elevating name-circulation from the level of sentient beings to the level of all buddhas.
- **The hearing-the-name outreach cluster** — vows 34 (forbearance of the unarisen dharma), 36 (constant celibacy), 37 (reverence from gods and humans), 41 (intact faculties), 42 (pure-liberation-*samādhi*), 43 (birth in noble families), 44 (accomplishment of root-virtues), 45 (universally-equal-*samādhi*), 47 (non-retrogression), 48 (three dharma-forbearances). Together these ten vows form a systematic catalog of "benefits received by bodhisattvas of other worlds upon hearing the name." T0362 has only three scattered vows in this semantic neighborhood. This ten-vow outreach cluster is T0360's most distinctive structural innovation relative to T0362 — and §3.2 treats it as the second of the four expansion moves.

The vow-concluding formula differs slightly in rhetorical force. T0362 says "if this vow is attained, I will become a buddha; if not, I will not in the end become a buddha" (*zhōng bù zuò fó*). T0360 says "I will not take complete enlightenment" (*bù qǔ zhèngjué*). The former has greater rhetorical weight (emphatic *zhōng bù*); the latter is more formal. But structurally both are conditional-vow formulas, and the syntactic inheritance across the Amitābha lineage is unmistakable.

### 2.4 Vasubandhu's T1524: The Twenty-Nine Adornments and the Five Contemplation-Gates

Vasubandhu's *Upadeśa on the Sūtra of Immeasurable Life* (*Jìngtǔ lùn* / *Wǎngshēng lùn*; T1524), translated into Chinese by Bodhiruci in the Northern Wei, is a one-fascicle treatise. It is the earliest systematic Indian commentary on Amitābha's buddha-field — contemporaneous with the Chinese translation of T0360, and roughly a century or two before Tanluan and Daochuo.

Vasubandhu's analytical framework for the Land of Peace and Bliss is the **five contemplation-gates** (*pañca-smṛti-dvāra*):

> "What are the five contemplation-gates? First, the gate of reverential prostration. Second, the gate of praise. Third, the gate of resolve. Fourth, the gate of observation. Fifth, the gate of transference of merit."[^3]

The five gates correspond to bodily, vocal, mental, wisdom, and compassionate action — the paradigmatic pathway by which the practitioner approaches the Land of Peace and Bliss. For our architectural analysis the decisive gate is the **fourth — observation** (*vīkṣaṇa*) — which divides its object of contemplation into three kinds of adornment:

> "Observation is of three kinds. What three? First, observing the merit-adornments of that buddha-land. Second, observing the merit-adornments of Amitābha Buddha. Third, observing the merit-adornments of those bodhisattvas."[^4]

These three adornment-categories contain seventeen, eight, and four items respectively — a total of twenty-nine adornment-accomplishments.

- **Seventeen adornments of the buddha-land**: purity, magnitude, nature, formal characteristics, diverse things, wondrous colors, touch, adornment, rain, radiance, sound, principal, retinue, enjoyment, freedom-from-difficulties, great meaning-gate, all-requested.[^5]
- **Eight adornments of Amitābha Buddha**: seat, body, speech, mind, retinue, chief, master, unvain-abiding.[^6]
- **Four adornments of the bodhisattvas**: motionless body responding to the ten directions, transformation-body radiating light to end suffering, offering-tours reaching all great assemblies, upholding the Three Jewels in places without them.[^7]

Vasubandhu then performs a decisive synthesis:

> "Furthermore, the aforementioned merit-adornment-accomplishments of the buddha-land, of the Buddha, and of the bodhisattvas — these three kinds of accomplishment are adornments of the vow-mind, and are summarily gathered into one dharma-sentence. The one dharma-sentence is the purity-sentence. The purity-sentence is: 'The unconditioned dharma-body of true wisdom.' This purity has two kinds, one should know. First, the container-world purity. Second, the sentient-world purity. The container-world purity is: the aforementioned seventeen kinds of merit-adornment-accomplishments of the buddha-land. The sentient-world purity is: the aforementioned eight kinds of merit-adornment-accomplishments of the Buddha and four kinds of the bodhisattvas. Thus the one dharma-sentence contains these two kinds of purity, one should know."[^8]

**The key textual move.** Vasubandhu collapses the diversity of the twenty-nine adornments into "one dharma-sentence" — with "purity-sentence" as its name and "the unconditioned dharma-body of true wisdom" as its referent. Then he unfolds the one dharma-sentence into "two purities": container-world purity (seventeen kinds) and sentient-world purity (eight plus four, equalling twelve). **Seventeen plus twelve equals twenty-nine.** The dual-axis structure of world and living beings has here been given an explicit, paradigmatic, authoritative statement.

The direct implication for §III. T1524's seventeen-plus-eight-plus-four classification is **not** a vow-by-vow correspondence with the forty-eight — Vasubandhu is not working at the vow-level; he is working at the *accomplishment-level*. But the container-world / sentient-world dual axis is itself a distinction already available before Vasubandhu, drawn from the Abhidharma and Yogācāra vocabulary of world-classification (seen in the *Yogācārabhūmi*, the *Abhidharmakośa*, and related treatises). Vasubandhu applies this dual axis to the doctrinal content of Amitābha's land. §3.1 works in the reverse direction: it takes this dual-axis analytical tool from Vasubandhu's commentary and looks back at the T0360 vow-text, finding that the text itself is already organized along that same dual axis. This is not a case of T0360 borrowing from T1524 (the chronology forbids it); it is a case of **both texts being rooted in the same Abhidharmic-Yogācāra dual-axis structure**. The dual axis is deep, not surface.

**Domain delimitation.** This paper cites T1524 only for its twenty-nine-adornment classificatory function and its "two purities" synthesizing statement. T1524 as a broader Yogācāra-affiliated treatise — on *ālaya-vijñāna*, *tathatā*, *dharma-kāya* as comprehensive doctrinal themes — falls under P17 and P18 (the *Laṅkāvatāra* and *Ghanavyūha* chapters), and is not taken up here.

### 2.5 Tanluan's T1957: The Two Purities (17 + 12)

Tanluan (476–542), a decisive commentarial figure between T0360's translation and Daochuo's systematization, led teaching at Xuanzhong Monastery in Bingzhou during the late Northern Wei. His *Abridged Treatise on the Meaning of the Land of Peace and Bliss* (T1957) is an independent work from his more famous *Commentary on the Sūtra of Rebirth* (T1819, not available in the present research canon). Only T1957 is cited here.

T1957 opens with a question-and-answer on the ontological status of the Land of Peace and Bliss:

> "Question: Among the three realms, to which realm does the Land of Peace and Bliss belong? Answer: As the *Dà zhì dù lùn* says — 'such a pure land is not contained within the three realms.' Why? Because there is no desire, it does not belong to the desire-realm; because there is dwelling on ground, it does not belong to the form-realm; because there is form, it does not belong to the formless realm … it is attained through separate karma, not through the three realms."[^9]

Tanluan then continues Vasubandhu's line and advances a critical formulation:

> "Question: How many kinds of adornments are in the Land of Peace and Bliss, that make it called the Pure Land? Answer: If one follows the sūtra in accordance with its meaning, the forty-eight vows of Dharmākara Bodhisattva are that very matter — by searching and praising one will understand, and I need not restate. If one follows the *Sūtra of Immeasurable Life Treatise*, by the two purities are gathered the twenty-nine kinds of merit-adornment-accomplishments. The two purities are: first, the container-world purity, and second, the sentient-world purity."[^10]

**This is the paper's central textual citation.** Tanluan explicitly juxtaposes two framings for describing "the adornments of the Land of Peace and Bliss":

- **Following the sūtra by meaning**: Dharmākara Bodhisattva's forty-eight vows.
- **Following the *Sūtra of Immeasurable Life Treatise***: Vasubandhu's two purities / twenty-nine adornments.

The first is the vow-level framing in T0360 (vow-language). The second is the adornment-level framing in T1524 (accomplishment-language). Both point to the same structural reality — namely the doctrinal content of the Land of Peace and Bliss — through two different surfaces. Tanluan's gesture is itself an architectural move. He explicitly says either framing is sufficient ("searching and praising one will understand, and I need not restate"), but he chooses Vasubandhu's adornment framing as the primary analytical tool — because the classificatory structure of the adornment frame is clearer.

Tanluan then enumerates the two purities in detail. His classification is **broadly consistent with Vasubandhu but not identical**:

- **Seventeen container-world adornments**: the buddha-land's aspect surpasses the three realms; the land is vast as space; the land arises from the bodhisattva's great-compassion right-path; radiance and light are complete; the land possesses the nature of precious jewels; it is pure and illuminates the worlds at all times; the touch of its treasures is soft and delightful; jewel-flowers, lotus-ponds, jewel-trees, and jewel-nets; celestial flowers, celestial robes, celestial fragrances rain from the sky; the Buddha's wisdom-light disperses the darkness of ignorance; Brahmā-voices open understanding, heard in ten directions; Amitābha as supreme dharma-king's excellent power sustains all; beings are born through the transformation of the Tathāgata's pure-lotus; food is the taste of dharma, meditative absorption, and *samādhi*; bodily and mental suffering are forever absent; the words "two-vehicles," "women," and "lacking faculties" are not heard; whatever beings desire and delight in accords with their mind.[^11]
- **Twelve sentient-world adornments**: the immeasurable great-jewel-king's wondrous flower-throne as the Buddha's seat; the body adorned with immeasurable marks and light; the Buddha's eloquence responding to the aptitudes of beings; the Buddha's thusness-wisdom illuminating general and specific marks; the unmoving host of gods and humans widely arrayed; the attainment of the supreme fruit, unrivaled; revered as the male-lion among gods and humans by the great assembly; the Buddha's original-vow-power-adornment sustaining all so as to quickly fulfill the ocean of merit — these are the **eight of the dharma-king**. The bodhisattvas of the Land of Peace and Bliss, motionless in body yet universally responding in all directions; their transformation-body radiating light in a single thought to end the suffering of beings; touring to all buddha-assemblies to make offerings, reverence, and praise; and upholding the Three Jewels in places where they are absent — these are the **four of the bodhisattvas**.[^12]

**Analytical note.** Tanluan adopts **"twelve sentient-world adornments"** as the upper-tier category, combining Vasubandhu's "eight of the Buddha + four of the bodhisattvas" into twelve. This choice sharpens the dual-axis structure: *seventeen-versus-twelve* is a balanced binary; *seventeen-versus-eight-versus-four* is a three-way asymmetry. The binary classification clearly opposes **world / living beings** as the first cut, and subdivides Buddha-bodhisattva-inhabitants only at a second level.

Why does Tanluan make this re-grouping? Because from the standpoint of **soteriological function**, both Buddha and bodhisattvas are sentient beings — they belong to the same ontological category as the inhabitants of the land (though the inhabitants are lower in status than the bodhisattvas). The first cut is land versus sentient beings. The internal hierarchy of Buddha, bodhisattvas, and inhabitants is a second-level substructure. Tanluan subordinates the second-level substructure to the first-level cut, making the dual axis visible at 17:12. §3.1 will follow this Tanluanian choice.

### 2.6 Daochuo's T1958: The Twelve Great Gates and the Five Great Benefits

Daochuo (562–645), in the *Collection on the Land of Peace and Bliss* (T1958), produces the signature systematization of early Tang Pure Land doctrine — a pivotal work between Tanluan and Shandao, laying the ground for Shandao's subsequent doctrinal closure.

Only two structural elements of T1958 are used here: **the outline of the twelve great gates** as an architectural signature, and the **five great benefits** passage as a sample of extractive citation of the vows. Other doctrinal themes — other-power, self-power, the *mòfǎ* (decline-of-dharma) periodization, and the ordinary-being-enters-reward-land doctrine — are reserved for P22 and Part VI.

Daochuo announces the overall architecture at the opening of T1958:

> "Within this *Collection on the Land of Peace and Bliss*, there are in one work altogether twelve great gates, all of which cite sūtras and treatises as proof, and encourage faith and seeking rebirth."[^13]

The twelve gates, in order, are: (1) the reasons for the teaching's arising; (2) arousing the bodhi-mind and refuting false attachments; (3) the difficult and the easy paths and long submersion in the three realms; (4) teacher-transmission and the *buddhānusmṛti-samādhi*; (5) prolongation of cultivation and the comparative evaluation of meditative contemplation; (6) the comparative ranking of the pure lands of the ten directions; (7) the binding and liberation of grasping marks here versus there; (8) the contrast of suffering and bliss; (9) long and short lifespans; (10) transference of merit; (11) the good spiritual friend and the superior versus inferior conditions of birth; (12) summary proof from the *Sūtra of the Ten Rebirths*.[^14] The twelve gates move from **doctrinal periodization** (gates 1–3) through **teacher-lineage and *buddhānusmṛti*** (gate 4) through **duration, contamination/non-contamination, pure-impure comparison** (gates 5–9) to **transference and the spiritual friend** (gates 10–11) and finally to **summarizing scriptural proof** (gate 12). Daochuo's systematization of the "why must we turn to the Pure Land?" question is more comprehensive than Tanluan's. It is not a simple question-and-answer sequence; it is a hierarchical doctrinal unfolding. (Daochuo also periodizes the present age, within gate one, as "the beginning of the age of the declining dharma" using the "five-times-five-hundred-years" scheme of the *Candragarbha Sūtra*; this is background only here, since the doctrinal dynamics of *mòfǎ* periodization belong to Part VI.)

Particularly relevant to this paper is the closing section of gate nine's first segment ("the opposition of suffering-bliss and good-evil"):

> "Further, citing the *Larger Sūtra*, within the forty-eight vows there are five great benefits: First, the *Larger Sūtra* says: 'If gods and humans of the ten directions come to be born in my land and are not all true-gold in color, I will not take complete enlightenment.' Second: 'If gods and humans of the ten directions come to be born in my land and differ in form with distinctions of beauty and ugliness, I will not take complete enlightenment.' Third: 'If gods and humans of the ten directions come to be born in my land and do not attain the knowledge of past lives, so as to know the matters of a hundred thousand koṭi nayuta kalpas, I will not take complete enlightenment.' Fourth: 'If gods and humans of the ten directions come to be born in my land and do not attain the divine ear, so as to hear and fully retain the teachings of a hundred thousand koṭi nayuta buddhas, I will not take complete enlightenment.' Fifth: 'If gods and humans of the ten directions come to be born in my land and do not attain the knowledge of others' minds, so as to know the mind-thoughts of beings in a hundred thousand koṭi nayuta buddha-lands, I will not take complete enlightenment.' To enumerate the beneficial matters of that land fully would be impossible."[^15]

This passage selects five vows from the forty-eight — corresponding to T0360's vows 3 (gold color), 4 (no beauty-ugliness differentiation), 5 (knowledge of past lives), 7 (divine ear), and 8 (knowledge of others' minds). These are called the "five great benefits" (*wǔ fān dà yì*). This is an **early Chinese paradigm of extractive citation of the forty-eight vows**.

**Observation.** All five vows chosen by Daochuo fall within the category **sentient-world: in-land — bodily marks and supernormal faculties** — a subset of the dual-axis table in §2.2. Daochuo's choice has its own rhetorical purpose (emphasizing the bodily and mental quality of the land's inhabitants), but the very fact of selection reveals category-consciousness. He is not picking five vows at random; he is extracting representative samples from a single category. This is an architectural signature of vow-use: *not* annotating every vow line-by-line (that becomes Shandao's task later), but extracting category-representative samples for doctrinal argumentation.

Daochuo also cites vow 18 ("even-ten-recollections in continuity") as the overall warrant for the other-power dominant-condition:

> "Therefore the *Larger Sūtra* says: 'Gods and humans of the ten directions who wish to be born in my land — none fail to take Tathāgata Amitābha's great-vow-action-power as the dominant condition.' If it were not so, the forty-eight vows would be set in vain."[^16]

This passage (in gate 3, first answer to the difficult-easy opposition) provides early-Tang paradigmatic testimony to **the architectural role of the eighteenth vow as threshold of the whole system**. Daochuo's statement is stark: without the eighteenth vow (and its built-in other-power dominant-condition mechanism), "the forty-eight vows would be set in vain." The forty-eight vows as an architecture **have the eighteenth vow as their enabling floor**. Without that floor, the other forty-seven vows have no beings to save, no path of access to execute. §3.4 unfolds this architectural implication.

**Domain delimitation.** The complete unfolding of Daochuo's *tālì* (other-power) doctrine, and the *mòfǎ* periodization with its "five-times-five-hundred" scheme, are here only background citations. Their full development belongs to P22 and Part VI. Daochuo's T1958 serves this paper in three functions: (a) the twelve-gate outline as an architectural signature just before the high Tang; (b) the five-great-benefits passage as a sample of extractive vow-citation; (c) the eighteenth-vow passage as paradigmatic location of "the Tathāgata's great-vow-action-power as the dominant condition."

## III. Core Argument

§II has established the textual ground in five scriptural and commentarial texts. §III now organizes this material into a coherent architectural argument, unfolded across four layers.

### 3.1 First Layer: The Dual Axis as Deep Structure

§2.2's classification table gives the category distribution of the forty-eight vows: 9 container-world vows against 39 sentient-world vows, a ratio of 1 to 4.33. §2.4's T1524 and §2.5's T1957 provide the paradigmatic statements of the dual axis — Vasubandhu's 17 + 8 + 4 and Tanluan's 17 + 12. Setting these three sets of numbers side by side:

| Source | Container-world | Sentient-world | Total | Ratio |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| T0360 vow-text (§2.2) | 9 | 39 | 48 | 1 : 4.33 |
| Vasubandhu's T1524 (29 adornments) | 17 | 12 (= 8 + 4) | 29 | 1 : 0.71 |
| Tanluan's T1957 (two purities) | 17 | 12 | 29 | 1 : 0.71 |

**The significance of the numerical contrast.** All three sources agree on the **dual axis** as the first-level division, but the **proportions differ dramatically** — the vow-text is heavily weighted toward sentient-world (1 : 4.33), while the adornment-system is roughly balanced (1 : 0.71).

Why do the vow-level and the adornment-level proportions diverge?

**The vow-text reflects a soteriological focus.** Dharmākara makes his vows in order to "rescue and deliver all beings" (the recapitulation verse: "I, through immeasurable kalpas, / would not be a great benefactor / and deliver the multitudes of poor and suffering, / if not, I swear I shall not become the fully enlightened"). The grammatical subject of the vows is therefore predominantly **beings** — their bodily marks, their supernormal faculties, their soteriological status, their conditions of access, the benefits they receive upon hearing the name. The land is only a container, not the ultimate end.

**The adornment-system reflects a contemplation focus.** Within Vasubandhu's five contemplation-gates, the **observation-gate** (*vīkṣaṇa-dvāra*) is a practice of meditative seeing. The fit object of such observation is "what can be observed by wisdom" — the concrete characteristics of the land (purity, magnitude, nature, formal characteristics, radiance, sound, retinue …) are more apt for observation than the abstract "mental conduct of beings." Accordingly, seventeen of the adornments are allocated to the land, twelve to the Buddha and the bodhisattvas. This proportion serves the **richness of phenomenal fields** required for contemplative operation, not the **soteriological focus** of vow-investment.

**Same axis, different proportions.** This is the first insight of the architectural analysis: the dual axis is the deep structure (the basic world/beings classification), but the proportional distribution within the axis varies with the functional purpose of the framing. The same structural reality appears in the vow-framing as soteriological asymmetry and in the adornment-framing as contemplative balance. The two surfaces do not contradict; they reflect two uses of the same underlying content — one designed for salvation, one designed for observation.

**Looking back at T0360 with this analytical tool in hand**, one finds inside the vow-text an implicit **three-ring structure**:

- **Container-world (outer ring)**: the conditions and ornaments of the land-container — 9 vows.
- **In-land beings (middle ring)**: the quality of residents within the container — Buddha 3, inhabitants 13, bodhisattvas 8 — total 24 vows.
- **Beings elsewhere (outer extension)**: beings outside the container affected by name, light, and vow-power — reception 3, hearing-name outreach 12 — total 15 vows.

The outer ring (land) acts as container → the middle ring (inhabitants) as beneficiaries → the outer extension (beings elsewhere) as soteriological reach. These three rings display T0360's vow-system as a **radiant structure**: from the innermost establishment of the land outward to the most distant beings. This radiant structure does **not hold** in T0362's twenty-four-vow version — T0362 has no large hearing-the-name outreach cluster (only a few scattered vows). T0360's forty-eight are not simply "T0362 doubled"; they are **T0362 plus a newly added outermost radiating extension**. §3.2 gives this observation a systematic statement.

### 3.2 Second Layer: Four Expansion Moves from T0362 to T0360

§2.3 has laid out the concrete vow-correspondences between T0362 (24 vows) and T0360 (48 vows). §3.2 organizes those correspondences into **four identifiable expansion moves**.

**Move 1: Granularization.**

Several items of compound vow-content in T0362 are split into multiple vows in T0360. Representative samples:

- T0362 vow 1 (no evil destinies + transformation-birth + growth into bodhisattva-arhat) → T0360 vow 1 (no hells, hungry ghosts, animals) + vow 2 (no falling back into the three evil destinies after death).
- T0362 vow 15 (purple-gold color + thirty-two marks + being-like-the-Buddha) → T0360 vow 3 (true-gold color) + vow 21 (thirty-two great-person marks).
- T0362 vow 4 (name-fame + praise of virtue + those hearing the name coming to be born) → T0360 vow 17 (all buddhas praise my name) + vow 18 (beings of the ten directions, even ten recollections) + vow 20 (sustained contemplation of the name and vow-power).

The logic of granularization is to **split compound content into atomic conditions**, so that each atomic condition is independently subject to the conditional-vow binding-force "if not so, I will not take complete enlightenment." Granularization is not bloat; it is **precision-raising**. T0362's single-vow compound structure becomes, in T0360, a one-vow-per-atomic-condition structure, which is categorically clearer.

**Move 2: New-category addition.**

Whole vow-categories not present in T0362 are added in T0360. The most striking is the **hearing-the-name outreach cluster** — the 12 vows in the "name/light outreach to beings elsewhere" row of §2.2's table. T0362 has only three scattered vows in this conceptual vicinity. T0360 expands this outreach domain from a handful of fragmented items into a systematic 12-vow cluster, running from **passive hearing of the name** (hearing-and-forbearance, hearing-and-constant-celibacy, hearing-and-reverence) to **active reception of soteriological effect** (hearing-and-intact-faculties, hearing-and-pure-liberation-*samādhi*, hearing-and-non-retrogression, hearing-and-three-forbearances).

This cluster is T0360's most architecturally distinctive innovation relative to T0362. P08 §2.2 has already noted T0361's seventeenth vow ("hearing the name") as the starting point of access-verb diversification; T0360 extends this starting point into a full catalog of hearing-the-name benefits, expanding "hearing the name" from a single access-verb into **twelve-fold spectrum of soteriological effects**.

Also notable among the new categories:

- **The stage of definite assurance** (vow 11). T0362 has no parallel. The Abhidharmic term *samyaktva-niyata-rāśi* (*sammatta-niyāma*) — the stage of definite stream-entry assurance — is imported into the Amitābha vow-system for the first time, directly linking "rebirth" to "confirmed bodhisattva stage." Once reborn in the land, one dwells in the stage of definite assurance and is destined for final cessation.
- **One-lifetime-remaining bodhisattva** (vow 22). T0362's closest analog is vow 7 (six-perfections + deathbed reception + attaining the non-retrogressing stage), which does not reach one-lifetime-remaining. T0360 vow 22 explicitly says "ultimately reaching the one-lifetime-remaining stage" and adds a Samantabhadra-practice exception clause, raising the fruition-hierarchy to its highest point.
- **The all-buddhas'-praise vow** (vow 17). T0362 vow 4 has an ambiguous subject; T0360 makes the subject explicit as "the innumerable buddhas of the ten directions," elevating the infrastructure of name-circulation from the level of sentient beings to the level of all buddhas.

**Move 3: Explicit access-mechanics.**

T0362's access-verbs are mostly implicit — "hearing the name and rejoicing and coming to be born"; "hearing the name and arousing the mind to come to be born"; "cultivating the six perfections and being received at death." T0360 makes these implicit access-conditions explicit:

- Vow 18: "even-ten-recollections" — a counting-delimited access unit (P08 §2.3 has analyzed this in detail).
- Vow 19: the conditions for deathbed reception — "arousing the bodhi-mind, cultivating various virtues, with a sincere heart making the vow to be born in my land" — a triple condition (aspiration + cultivation + vow).
- Vow 20: the conditions for sustained-contemplation assured birth — "hearing my name, sustaining the contemplation of my land, planting various root-virtues, with a sincere heart transferring merit and wishing to be born in my land" — a quadruple condition.
- Vow 11: "dwelling in the stage of definite assurance and reaching final cessation" — the soteriological fruit of rebirth specified in concrete stage-terminology.

The function of explicit access-mechanics is to shift the vow-system from **aspirational** (I *hope* beings can come) to **operational** (I *guarantee* that beings who fulfill these conditions can come). This shift endows the forty-eight vows with a **verifiable operational structure** — later commentators (Tanluan, Daochuo, Shandao) can then build systematic doctrinal edifices from these explicit mechanisms.

**Move 4: Verbal-circulation infrastructure.**

T0362's fourth vow fused "fame of name in the eight directions" with "praise of virtue" with "those who hear the name coming to be born" in one vow; T0360 separates "fame of name" out as an independent vow: vow 17, "all buddhas praise my name." The subject of this vow is **all buddhas**; the action is **praise**. This is T0360's first introduction of "praising the Buddha's name" as a cosmic-level event into the vow-system (P08 has noted that T0360 vow 17's subject is all buddhas, not sentient beings).

The functional role of this infrastructure: vow 18's premise, "beings of the ten directions who hear my name," requires that the name be circulating and spoken throughout the ten directions. Vow 17's "all buddhas praise my name" supplies this premise — the buddhas serve as a cosmic-level broadcasting network, making the Buddha's name accessible to beings in all directions. Without vow 17 as infrastructure, the "beings of the ten directions" scope of vow 18 would be logically unsupported.

T0360 thus **supports** the beings-level access-mechanism (even-ten-recollections) with the cosmic-level name-circulation infrastructure (all-buddhas'-praise). This is an elegant architectural move — the premise-conditions of the infrastructure are built into the vow-system itself, not outsourced.

**The four moves in combined perspective.** Granularization raises precision; new categories extend scope; explicit access-mechanics deliver operability; name-circulation establishes infrastructure. The four are not independent — they mutually support each other. Scope extension requires infrastructure build-out; operational explicitness requires atomic precision; precision-raising makes intra-category subdivision possible. The expansion from T0362 to T0360 is therefore not arithmetic doubling but **multi-dimensional architectural refinement**.

### 3.3 Third Layer: The Soteriological Asymmetry and Its Architectural Meaning

§3.1 has already observed that T0360's vow-system is heavily weighted toward sentient-world (1 : 4.33). §3.3 asks: **what is the structural meaning of this asymmetry?**

**Observation one.** Of the 39 sentient-world vows, 24 (nearly two-thirds) concern **beings within the land** — those already reborn. This means T0360's principal soteriological investment is in the **quality of those already reborn**, not in the **threshold of rebirth itself** (the latter has only three vows: 18, 19, 20). The vow-system implies a narrative: once access is achieved (through mechanisms such as the eighteenth vow), beings immediately enter a condition of radical upgrade — gold color, supernormal faculties, soteriological status, immeasurable lifespan — and this upgraded condition is itself the bulk of the soteriological operation.

**Observation two.** The remaining 15 vows (sentient-world: outreach) concern **beings elsewhere** — those not yet reborn or not yet having heard the name. These 15 vows consist of 3 reception vows and 12 hearing-the-name outreach vows. Together they define the **spectrum of transition from not-yet-reborn to reborn** — lowest threshold is simply hearing the name (vow 41, intact faculties); next lowest is hearing the name and arousing a certain intention (vow 35, female-to-male transformation; vow 37, reverence from gods and humans); next is hearing the name plus sustained contemplation plus transference of merit (vow 20); the lowest formal threshold for proper access is "sincere joyful faith + wish to be born + even-ten-recollections" (vow 18).

Combining the two observations: **T0360's vow-system invests 24 vows in those already reborn, 15 in those in transition, and 9 in the container itself**. The 24:15:9 investment proportion reflects a soteriological architecture with a clear priority ordering:

- **Highest priority**: the buddhahood-attainment of those already reborn (may they swiftly attain bodhi).
- **Middle priority**: the access conditions and benefits of those in transition (may they be able to come, may they receive benefit).
- **Lowest priority**: the texture of the container itself (may the land bear the soteriological operation).

This priority structure **inverts** a popular image of the Pure Land. In common imagination, "the Pure Land" is characterized by landscape splendor (seven jewels, jewel-trees, water of the eight virtues …), as if the point of the Pure Land were environmental magnificence. But T0360's vow-investment profile shows: **the environment is only a container**. The beauty of the land serves to "allow the soteriological operation to run smoothly," not to "provide those already reborn or beings elsewhere with an experience of beauty" as an end in itself.

**This resonates deeply with Tanluan's move in §2.5.** Tanluan said that "Dharmākara's forty-eight vows" and "Vasubandhu's two purities" are two framings of the same structural reality. But the two framings have different investment profiles: the vow-framing is 1 : 4.33 weighted toward sentient-world (soteriological focus); the adornment-framing is 1 : 0.71, balanced between container and beings (contemplation focus). The two are complementary rather than contradictory — one designed for salvation (vow), one designed for observation (adornment).

**Reinforcement for the three-ring radiant structure of §3.1.** The investment proportion of the three rings (container-world, in-land beings, beings elsewhere) of 9 : 24 : 15 yields an **onion-shaped soteriological geometry** — the outermost layer (container) is thinnest, the middle layer (in-land beings as beneficiaries) is thickest, the outermost extension (beings elsewhere as reach) is next-thickest. This geometry implies that T0360's architecture is **designed for operation** — the middle layer is thick because the soteriological operation takes place there; the outer extension is thick because the operation requires raw material to touch; the outermost layer is thin because the container itself is not the site of operation.

### 3.4 Fourth Layer: Vow Eighteen as the Architectural Threshold (Floor, Not Pinnacle)

§3.3 has established that the vow-system invests only three vows (18, 19, 20) in access-conditions. §3.4 asks: **what is the architectural role of these three vows? What, specifically, is the structural meaning of the eighteenth vow?**

**The conditional ladder of the three access-vows:**

| Vow | Condition | Ladder Position |
|---|---|---|
| Vow 19 | arouse bodhi-mind + cultivate various virtues + sincerely make the vow to be born | Highest (requires bodhi-mind + virtues + vow) |
| Vow 20 | hear the name + sustain contemplation + plant root-virtues + sincerely transfer merit + wish to be born | Middle (requires hearing + contemplation + merit-transfer) |
| Vow 18 | sincerely joyful faith + wish to be born + even-ten-recollections (except those who commit the five grave offenses and slander the true dharma) | Lowest (requires sincerity + wish + ten recollections) |

The three vows descend in demand-level — vow 19 has the highest threshold (bodhi-mind + virtues), vow 20 is intermediate, vow 18 is lowest. The eighteenth vow's condition is **the lowest threshold** (minimal condition) of the access-mechanism.

At the same time, the audience of the eighteenth vow is **"beings of the ten directions"** — not restricted to those who have aroused the bodhi-mind (as in vow 19), not restricted to those already capable of sustained contemplation of the Buddha-land (as in vow 20), but the whole of the ten-directional sentient world. The audience is maximal.

**Minimum condition + maximum audience = the architectural threshold of the whole system.** The threshold is not the pinnacle — not the system's highest attainment, not its shortcut — but the **enabling node** that allows the system to reach the largest possible population of beings. Without vow 18's minimum threshold, the system cannot touch the beings who cannot satisfy vow 19's or vow 20's conditions; and in any plausible demographic of the ten-directional sentient world, that group is the overwhelming majority. This is especially so in the Chinese reading that crystallizes from Daochuo onward, which foregrounds the "dull-capacity beings of the declining dharma age" as the paradigmatic audience.

**Paradigmatic confirmation in Daochuo.** §2.6 has already cited Daochuo's T1958: "Gods and humans of the ten directions who wish to be born in my land — none fail to take Tathāgata Amitābha's great-vow-action-power as the dominant condition. If it were not so, the forty-eight vows would be set in vain." The architectural meaning of this statement is: **the eighteenth vow (and its built-in other-power dominant-condition mechanism) is the enabling condition that gives the whole forty-eight-vow architecture a population of beings to save**. Without the eighteenth vow's minimum threshold, the high-requirement conditions of the other vows would exclude most beings, and the architectural reach — especially the outermost radiation of the hearing-the-name outreach cluster — would be structurally unreachable.

**Connection to P08's *niàn*-*chēng* coupling.** P08 §3.2 has analyzed T0360 vow 18's "even-ten-recollections" as a semantic hinge — counting-delimited (ten occurrences), object-anchored (recollecting Buddha of Immeasurable Life), verb-undetermined (both *niàn* as mental holding and *chēng* as vocal utterance remain live). The architectural correlate of this verbal-analytical hinge is precisely the access-threshold analyzed in this section. *Why must the hinge remain undetermined — reserving possibilities for both **niàn** and **chēng**?* Precisely because it is the floor: a threshold must accommodate the maximum range of beings and cannot presuppose that the practitioner must be capable of *niàn* (mental holding) specifically, or of *chēng* (vocal utterance) specifically, to cross. The semantic openness of the threshold serves its architectural function as the minimum threshold of access. **P08's verbal hinge and this paper's architectural floor are two descriptive dimensions of the same fact** — one about the flexibility of verb-choice, the other about the minimality of condition-requirement.

### 3.5 Summary: The Forty-Eight Vows as a Designed Architecture

Combining the four layers:

1. **Deep structure**: the container-world / sentient-world dual axis (Vasubandhu 17 + 8 + 4, Tanluan 17 + 12, T0360 vow-text 9 : 39).
2. **Expansion logic**: four moves from T0362 to T0360 (granularization, new-category addition, explicit access-mechanics, name-circulation infrastructure).
3. **Investment proportion**: 9 : 24 : 15 (container : in-land beings : beings elsewhere), implying an onion-shaped geometry of container, beneficiaries, and reach.
4. **Access-threshold**: vow 18 as the enabling node of minimum condition and maximum audience.

The forty-eight vows are therefore not a list of forty-eight parallel promises but a designed architecture with **deep classificatory structure, a reconstructible expansion trajectory, a soteriological investment priority, and an enabling access-threshold**. This architecture takes soteriology as its *telos*, the dual axis as its skeleton, radiant expansion as its spatial logic, and vow 18 as its enabling access.

## IV. Cross-Disciplinary Engagement: Design Theory and Dharmākara's Vow-Ocean

The proposal that the forty-eight vows are a designed architecture opens space for dialogue with contemporary design theory. Two figures from the second half of the twentieth century make productive interlocutors: **Christopher Alexander** (architect, principal author of *A Pattern Language*; Alexander, Ishikawa, and Silverstein, *A Pattern Language: Towns, Buildings, Construction*, Oxford University Press, 1977) and **Herbert Simon** (a polymath across economics, cognitive science, and computer science; author of *The Sciences of the Artificial*, MIT Press, 1969; third edition 1996).[^17]

### 4.1 Simon and the Ontology of the Designed Object

Simon's core claim in *The Sciences of the Artificial*: there is a class of scientific objects whose nature is not that of natural kinds but of **artificial things** — made by human making and designed to accomplish some purpose. Simon argues that the science of such objects (which he calls "design science") has three structural elements:

1. **Inner environment**: the internal structure, materials, and components of the object.
2. **Outer environment**: the context in which the object is designed to operate, the requirements of the task, the external constraints.
3. **Interface**: the adaptive mechanism between inner and outer — the structure that allows the inner to meet the needs of the outer.

Simon's paradigmatic example is the computer: the inner environment is hardware plus software, the outer environment is the tasks the user needs to accomplish, and the interface is the layer through which the user interacts with the system. Good design is the condition in which the interface allows the inner environment to meet the outer environment's task requirements effectively.

Applied to the forty-eight vows:

- **Inner environment**: the internal structure of the forty-eight vows — §3.1's dual-axis classification, §3.2's four expansion moves, §3.3's 9 : 24 : 15 investment proportion, §3.4's access-threshold.
- **Outer environment**: the soteriological task — beings need to escape from the suffering of *saṃsāra*, attain non-retrogressing bodhisattva status, and ultimately attain buddhahood. This outer environment is co-constituted by the situation of beings in the ten directions, the cosmic context of the buddha-fields in all directions, and the concrete condition of the "five-turbid evil age."
- **Interface**: the access mechanism — vow 18's even-ten-recollections, vow 19's deathbed reception, vow 20's sustained-contemplation transfer. This interface converts the effort of beings (arrayed at different condition-levels) into the soteriological response of the system (rebirth, non-retrogression, eventual buddhahood).

Reading the forty-eight vows in Simon's terms, one sees them as **an artificial object designed for a soteriological task**. The value of this reading lies in the fact that it shifts the question "why do the forty-eight vows take this particular shape?" from the level of *history / psychology* (why did Dharmākara choose these vows rather than others?) to the level of *structure / function* (what outer environment shaped this inner structure as its design?). The latter is more tractable because the outer environment is something that the doctrinal tradition can make explicit: the concrete situation of the ten-directional beings, the cosmic condition of the five-turbid evil age.

### 4.2 Alexander and Pattern Language

Alexander's core claim in *A Pattern Language*: **good design is not produced top-down but is composed from recurring patterns**. A pattern is a structural pair of "a recurring problem + a recurring solution" — for example, the "entrance transition" pattern describes a recurring problem (people moving from public space to private space need psychological transition) and offers a recurring solution (at the entrance, design a transition zone in which the entering person can psychologically reset).

Alexander's *A Pattern Language* collects 253 such patterns, organized as a hierarchical network from the urban scale (towns, communities of seven thousand) through the architectural scale (the bench at the front door) to the detail scale (edge conditions). No single pattern solves everything; only multiple patterns in coordinated combination constitute a complete design response.

Applied to the forty-eight vows: §3.2's four expansion moves can be read as **patterns**:

- **The granularization pattern**. Recurring problem: compound conditions cannot be clearly satisfied or violated. Recurring solution: split compound conditions into atomic-condition vows, so that each atomic condition can be verified independently. This pattern is applied in T0362 vow 1 → T0360 vows 1 + 2; T0362 vow 15 → T0360 vows 3 + 21; and so on.
- **The access-ladder pattern**. Recurring problem: beings of different capacities need different condition-levels of access. Recurring solution: design different access-vows at different levels — vow 18 (floor), vow 20 (middle), vow 19 (top).
- **The infrastructure-support pattern**. Recurring problem: some vows depend on prior conditions. Recurring solution: set up supporting vows (such as vow 17, all-buddhas'-praise) to supply those prior conditions.
- **The fruition-guarantee pattern**. Recurring problem: how to ensure that those reborn will not regress. Recurring solution: install non-retrogression guarantees (vow 11's stage of definite assurance; vow 22's one-lifetime-remaining).

The forty-eight vows can therefore be read as **a collection within a pattern language**, with each vow (or cluster of vows) instantiating a recurring design-pattern, and together forming a pattern-network of soteriological architecture. The significance of this reading is that it lets us see **the non-arbitrariness of the internal structure of the forty-eight vows** — why these forty-eight vows rather than any arbitrary forty-eight? Because these vows instantiate a series of recurring patterns, each pattern addressing a recurring soteriological problem.

### 4.3 ⚠️ Three Non-Reducible Differences

Following the same heuristic pattern as P06 (Thompson's phenomenology), P07 (Kripke's and Lewis's modal metaphysics), and P08 (Austin's and Searle's speech-act theory) — contemporary frameworks as pairing partners, not as theoretical replacements — three structural boundaries must be marked.

**Teleological difference.** Alexander's and Simon's design theory serves human practical ends — architecture, urban planning, organizational design, systems engineering. The ultimate purpose of design is the satisfaction of the user's practical, aesthetic, or functional needs. The ultimate purpose of the forty-eight vows is **soteriological** — the root liberation of beings, escape from *saṃsāra*, attainment of buddhahood. This ultimate purpose is not an extension of user-need-satisfaction; it is a radically different order of concern — involving the ontological structures of dependent origination (*pratītyasamutpāda*), karma, and the soteriological path. To reduce the forty-eight vows' ultimate design-purpose to practical task-satisfaction would miss the entire core of Mahāyāna soteriology.

**Ontological difference.** The ontology of Alexander's and Simon's design-objects depends on **material causation + human intention + social convention**. A building depends on the physical causation of brick and concrete, the architect's intention, and the social convention that "this is a habitable structure." The ontology of the forty-eight vows depends on **vow-power + dependent origination + the dominant condition** (*adhipati-pratyaya*). The performative power of the vow is not constituted by physical causation, is not constituted by human intention (Dharmākara's intention is one of the conditions, not the sole cause), and is not constituted by social convention. It is constituted by vow-power itself as a structural component in the architecture of dependent origination — what Daochuo calls "Tathāgata Amitābha's great-vow-action-power as the dominant condition" is the Chinese formulation of this ontological status. To reduce the vow to the intention of a designer is to eliminate vow-ontology at a stroke.

**Upgrade-path difference.** Alexander's and Simon's design has completion conditions — when the design meets its functional criteria, it is complete. A building is constructed, a system is deployed, a pattern language is documented, and the design work ends. The design itself has no upgrade path; it is a descriptive, prescriptive tool. The forty-eight vows, as a designed object, **have an explicit upgrade path** — the establishment of the vow is only the enabling beginning of a soteriological trajectory. Once beings enter the Land of Peace and Bliss through the access mechanism, they must still traverse the long trajectory of non-retrogression, one-lifetime-remaining, full buddhahood, and the eventual establishment of their own buddha-fields (cf. vow 22's one-lifetime-remaining clause). This trajectory cannot be generated from within design theory; it cannot be substituted by it. Design theory's analysis of the vow-system **as a structure** is valid; the analysis of the vow-system **as a trajectory** must return to the internal framework of Mahāyāna soteriology.

### 4.4 A Special Caution for Western Readers

Recent decades have seen a strand of cross-disciplinary philosophical work that tends to read Buddhist cosmology as "ancient systems engineering" — lifting the complex Buddhist cosmological architectures, from the Western reader's viewpoint, out of "ancient religious imagination" and into "formally structured design work." This move is partly warranted: the forty-eight vows do have the structural logic of a designed architecture, and the Simon-Alexander frameworks can illuminate the internal structure. But taken too far, the move reduces vow-ontology to the particularity of engineered artifacts.

This paper uses Alexander and Simon only as heuristic interlocutors — to let Western readers see that the forty-eight vows are neither "arbitrary promise-lists from an ancient religion" nor "animistic projections of religious imagination," but structured constructions with recognizable design-logic — without reducing the vows to engineered artifacts. The three non-reducible differences (*telos*, ontology, upgrade path) are the structural boundary.

This caution follows the same methodological pattern as P06's Thompson-dialogue, P07's Kripke-Lewis-dialogue, P08's Austin-Searle-dialogue — the same methodological structure applied to a different domain. The forty-eight vows are not one philosophical object among many; they are a soteriological construct whose design-logic can dialogue with contemporary design theory without thereby being derivative of it.

## V. Middle-Way Correction

**❌ Correction 1.** *The forty-eight vows are a list of forty-eight promises.* — Mistaken. The four-layer analysis in §III has already shown that the forty-eight vows are an architecture, not a flat list. Deep structure (dual axis), expansion trajectory (four moves), investment proportion (9 : 24 : 15), and access-threshold (vow 18) — these four structural features together exclude the flat-list reading. Reading the forty-eight vows as a list misses the following facts: (a) certain vows (such as vow 17, all-buddhas'-praise) are **infrastructure vows**, whose function is to support other vows (the premise of vow 18); (b) certain vows (such as vow 11, stage of definite assurance; vow 22, one-lifetime-remaining) are **guarantee vows**, whose function is to secure the soteriological status of those already reborn; (c) certain vows (such as vows 18, 19, 20) are **access vows**, whose function is to define the entry-conditions for beings of different capacities. These three functional differentiations can be seen only under the architectural reading.

**❌ Correction 2.** *The forty-eight vows are the arbitrary expression of Dharmākara's personal will.* — Mistaken. P07 §3.3 has already analyzed the ontology of Dharmākara's vow-making: Lokeśvararāja Buddha **first displays the catalog of 210 billion buddha-lands**, and Dharmākara, after **five kalpas of meditation**, then **gathers the pure practices of the buddha-lands**. The forty-eight vows are not produced by Dharmākara ex nihilo; they are **selected and combined** from the existing property-inventory of 210 billion buddha-lands. This selection-combination ontology makes the "arbitrary personal will" reading structurally untenable — Dharmākara's action is one of selection from a catalog, not creation from scratch. Moreover, §2.3's T0362-vs-T0360 comparison shows that the content of the forty-eight vows has its own diachronic refinement trajectory (granularization, new categories, access-mechanics, infrastructure) — which further excludes the arbitrariness reading, since arbitrary expression does not display identifiable trajectories.

**❌ Correction 3.** *Among the forty-eight vows, vow 18 is the most important; the other forty-seven are secondary.* — Mistaken. Vow 18 is the **access-threshold** of the architecture, not its pinnacle, and certainly not a substitute for the other forty-seven vows. Reading vow 18 as the sole important vow collapses the entire dual-axis-and-three-ring radiant structure — container-world vows, in-land-being vows, and hearing-the-name outreach vows would all be demoted to the status of appendages. Shandao's T1753, in the five-right-practice framework, explicitly treats **recitation, observation, reverence, vocal invocation, and praise-offering as equally parallel** right-practices (P08 §2.5 has cited this), and this framework itself resists the reading of vow 18 as the single center. The architectural position of vow 18 is the enabling threshold — it allows the other forty-seven vows to reach beings, but it does not replace the concrete soteriological content of those other forty-seven vows. The later doctrinal development in Japanese Jōdo Shinshū of elevating vow 18 to "the fundamental vow" (*hongan*) while treating the other vows as "expedient" (*hōben*) is a sectarian elaboration of a later period, not the native reading of the T0360 paradigm text itself.

**⚠️ Caution 1.** The Alexander-Simon design-theoretic dialogue is heuristic, not theoretically equivalent. The three non-reducible differences (*telos*, ontology, upgrade path) constitute structural boundaries. In particular, Simon's "design science" framework, if applied directly to the forty-eight vows, loses **the ontological status of vow-power as the dominant condition** — the performative power of the vow is not "designer intention plus social convention," but the operation of the dominant condition (*adhipati-pratyaya*) as one class of forces within the architecture of dependent origination. Daochuo's "Tathāgata Amitābha's great-vow-action-power as the dominant condition" is the Chinese summative formulation of this ontology, not absorbable into any engineered-artifact framework.

**⚠️ Caution 2.** §3.1 and §2.5 use Vasubandhu's 17 + 8 + 4 (T1524) and Tanluan's 17 + 12 (T1957) as textual evidence for the dual-axis structure, but the **two classifications differ slightly**. Vasubandhu keeps "8 Buddha + 4 bodhisattva" as two separate categories; Tanluan merges them into "12 sentient-world purities." §3.1 adopts Tanluan's 17 + 12 (which more clearly displays the dual axis), but this is an analytical choice, not a judgment of scriptural orthodoxy. Readers who prefer Vasubandhu's three-way classification (land, Buddha, bodhisattva) can still accept the dual-axis argument of §3.1 — they need only foreground the internal subdivision of 12 (Buddha 8, bodhisattva 4) at a second hierarchical level. The surface difference is real; the deep structure is the same.

**⚠️ Caution 3.** This paper argues that the forty-eight vows are a designed architecture but does not argue how this architecture has been differently instantiated in practice across the Chinese sectarian lineages. Tanluan, Daochuo, and Shandao form the Northern-Wei-to-early-Tang systematization lineage; Zhiyi's four kinds of *buddhānusmṛti* in Tiantai, Fazang's Pure Land perspective in Huayan, the vocalized *buddhānusmṛti* of Chan, and the Ming-dynasty convergence of Chan and Pure Land — all these later reception-history dynamics belong to Part VI (P20–P24) as specialized treatment. The present paper works only with the architecture centered on T0360; it does not endorse any specific reception-history narrative.

## VI. Conclusion

**Summary.** The forty-eight vows are not a list of forty-eight promises but a designed architecture — with soteriology as its ultimate purpose, the container-world / sentient-world dual axis as deep structure, the four expansion moves from T0362 to T0360 as its diachronic refinement trajectory, the 9 : 24 : 15 three-ring investment proportion as its radiant geometry, and vow 18 as the enabling access-threshold. Vasubandhu's T1524 (17 + 8 + 4 = 29 adornments) and Tanluan's T1957 (17 + 12, the two purities) provide paradigmatic dual-axis tools; Daochuo's T1958 (twelve great gates, five great benefits) provides the Chinese architectural signatures of extractive vow-citation. This architectural reading of the forty-eight vows displaces the flat-list reading of "forty-eight independent promises" — revealing functional differentiation (infrastructure vows, guarantee vows, access vows, bodily-mark vows, supernormal-faculty vows, hearing-the-name outreach vows), revealing the expansion trajectory (from T0362's twenty-four vows through granularization and new-category addition), revealing the architectural position of the access-threshold. Christopher Alexander's pattern language and Herbert Simon's sciences of the artificial provide frameworks for dialogue with contemporary design theory, but the vow's ontology of the dominant condition, its ultimate soteriological purpose, and its upgrade-path trajectory are non-interchangeable with design theory.

**Practical guidance.** For contemporary readers — Western and Chinese alike — there is a concrete implication. Facing the forty-eight vows, one need not read them as "forty-eight shopping promises" (whichever vow one purchases, that is the benefit one receives), nor as "the collected personal list of Dharmākara." They are **an architecture designed for salvation**. The position a practitioner occupies in this architecture is not "shopping for one vow," but entering **the appropriate position within the dual-axis structure**. If one is a being in other worlds who has not yet heard the name, vow 17's name-circulation infrastructure is already operating on one's behalf; if one has heard the name, the cluster of vows 33 through 48 (hearing-the-name outreach) corresponds to one's concrete situation; if the wish to be born has already arisen, vows 18, 19, and 20 present the access-ladder at different requirement-levels; if one has already been reborn, the in-land-beings cluster of vows 3 through 22 describes one's new status. To read the vow-system is not to select a preferred vow but to recognize one's current position within the architecture, and to use that position as starting point for interaction with the system. This framing excludes neither name-invocation practice, nor the sixteen contemplations, nor any other Chinese Buddhist practice — it places concrete practices back into the larger map of T0360's paradigm architecture.

**Open question.** This paper has treated T0360's forty-eight vows as a vow-level architecture and has cited Vasubandhu's T1524 and Tanluan's T1957 adornment-level frameworks as dual-axis tools. But the architectural foundation of the Three Pure Land Sūtras has another dimension that has not yet been addressed: the **sixteen contemplations** of the *Sūtra on the Contemplation of Amitāyus* (T0365). The sixteen contemplations constitute an architecture at the level of **observation and visualization** — beginning with foundational contemplations of the sun, water, and ground, deepening through contemplations of jewel-trees, waters of the eight virtues, the flower-seat, and the true body, moving through Avalokiteśvara and Mahāsthāmaprāpta to the universal contemplation, and finally fanning out into the concrete marks of the nine grades of rebirth. If T0360 is the vow-level soteriological architecture, T0365 is the contemplation-level cognitive-visual architecture — they are not redundant descriptions of the same thing, but two complementary architectural approaches to the one underlying reality of the Land of Peace and Bliss. The next paper, P10, will address the architectural logic of the sixteen contemplations of T0365 — in particular, why the sixteen are arranged in this specific sequence, what cognitive geometry underlies their trajectory from external (sun, water, ground) to internal (true body, universal contemplation), and how the sixteen converge into the nine grades of rebirth as their access-point of differentiation. After P10, P11 addresses the description of Sukhāvatī in the *Shorter Sukhāvatīvyūha Sūtra* (T0366) and the final access-convergence of "holding the name" (*zhípí míng-hào*). Only by reading all three sūtras together can one obtain the full picture of the Pure Land paradigm architecture.

*Namo tassa bhagavato arahato sammāsambuddhassa.*
