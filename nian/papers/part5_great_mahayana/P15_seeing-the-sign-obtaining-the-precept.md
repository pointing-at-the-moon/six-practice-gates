# Seeing the Sign, Obtaining the Precept

**The *Brahma Net* Bodhisattva Precepts and the Precept-Dependent Verbal Grammar of Sustained Buddha-Recollection — From the Five-Layer Structure of the Ten Major and Forty-Eight Minor Precepts to Fazang's Ontology of the Precept-Body**

## Abstract

This paper takes up the fourth member of Part V's Mahāyāna-Eight-Sūtras cluster — the *Brahma Net Sūtra* (*Fànwǎng jīng* 梵網經, T1484) — arguing three things. **First**, T1484 weaves precept-keeping and buddha-recollection as *equal-weight dual axes* through a five-layer structure of sustained contact with all buddhas: the sixth minor precept's thrice-daily reverence (the micro-rhythmic foundation), the twenty-third minor precept's self-vowed precept-reception verified by the *seeing of auspicious signs* (*jiàn hǎoxiàng* 見好相, the initiating event), the thirty-seventh minor precept's bimonthly *poṣadha* recitation before buddha-and-bodhisattva images (the collective synchronization), the forty-first minor precept's offense-atonement through night-and-day six-hour precept recitation and exhausting prostrations before the buddhas of the three times to attain a sign (the restoration mechanism), and the five benefits verse's *seeing the thousand buddhas hand-conferring at the moment of death* (the terminal recovery). Five layers on distinct temporal scales converge on one ground-action — the precept-body activated before buddha-images, cultivated through accumulative action, completed or redeemed through the appearance of auspicious signs. **Second**, against the Yogācāra Indian counterpart (*Bodhisattva-prātimokṣa*, T1501, Xuanzang trans.) with its four *pārājayika* and forty-three minor precepts — where only the first minor precept's "at the very least, with a single mind of pure faith, recollect the true qualities of the three jewels" (*xià zhì yǐ xīn yī qīngjìng xìn suíniàn sānbǎo zhēnshí gōngdé* 下至以心一清淨信隨念三寶真實功德) articulates the inward minimum threshold of daily recollection — T1484's structural expansion arises from two pressures: accommodating lay precept-receivers lacking the foundational śrāvaka *prātimokṣa*, and addressing the absence of the Buddha in the degenerate age. The precept-body is thereby recast from *intention-pacification* to *continuous contact with the Buddha's presence through embodied form*. **Third**, Fazang's *Commentary on the Bodhisattva Precept Section of the Brahma Net Sūtra* (*Fànwǎng-jīng púsà jièběn shū* 梵網經菩薩戒本疏, T1813) treats this precept-body through *ontological-structural processing*: the source-ontology of Rocana as the original body with thousand-hundred-million emanation-Śākyamunis; the three-fold negation of precept-essence (*lí sè-xīn, lí yǒu-wú, lí yīn-guǒ* 離色心、離有無、離因果 — apart from form-and-mind, apart from being-and-non-being, apart from cause-and-effect); and the self-vowed precept-reception's auspicious-sign correspondence as the sole event-description of "contact-with-the-Buddha" in the entire commentary. Against Guifeng Zongmi's *mind-dialectical processing* in P14 (*thinking-of-the-Buddha is also a samsaric mind* versus *bowing to the names of the ten-direction buddhas*; the four-disease metronome), Fazang represents the ontological-structural wing of the Huayan commentarial legacy — addressing *the container of recollection* rather than *the subject of recollection*. Jiāoguāng (P12) – Zongmi (P14) – Fazang (P15) together form the first three nodes of Part V's cross-commentator axis. **Grounding-in-precepts** (*yī-jiè* 依戒) constitutes the sixth layer of the NIAN verb-axis — extending buddha-recollection from *session-bounded action* to *whole-life-world sustained posture*; precept-keeping *is* continuous buddha-recollection, precept-body *is* the container of the primordial buddha. §VI closes with a forward link to P16's *Golden Light Sūtra* — the dual axis of precept (P15) and repentance (P16) structures the center of Part V.

## I. Introduction — When the Precepts Are Reduced to a Sheet of Rules

When Western anglophone Buddhist readers approach the *Brahma Net Sūtra*, two reading postures are most common. The first is **ethicization** — reading the bodhisattva precepts as a Buddhist version of moral code, or a checklist of "mindful-living" conduct: the precept against killing approximated to vegetarian choice, against sexual misconduct to relational ethics, against intoxicants to health planning. The second is **ritualization** — reading the bodhisattva precepts as a one-time ceremony of lineage transmission: ordination is a single credentialing event, the precept-certificate a document marking religious identity, and the subsequent keeping becomes a matter of free-form conscience.

Both readings demote **precept** from *sustained action* to *static attribute*. Ethicization treats precepts as lifestyle; ritualization treats precepts as identity-marker. Both share the premise that "once received, the precepts recede into the background" — an external norm available for reference, rather than an internal action requiring continual activation.

Yet the precept-architecture of the *Brahma Net Sūtra* is precisely a refutation of this premise. The sūtra defines the **precept-body** (*śīla-kāya*, *jiètǐ* 戒體) as "the luminous, adamantine-treasury precept, the origin of all buddhas, the origin of all bodhisattvas, the seed of buddha-nature."[^1] The precepts are not a collection of norms but a *sustained action bearing ontological luminosity*. What makes the precepts the precepts is not the memorization of clauses but the ceaseless re-activation of the precept-body. The mechanism of activation is given within the sūtra itself as **precept-keeping and buddha-recollection operating as equal-weight dual axes** — thrice-daily reverence, bimonthly *poṣadha* recitation, self-vowed reception verified by seeing auspicious signs, offense-atonement through bowing to the three-period thousand buddhas, and the deathbed vision of the thousand buddhas hand-conferring. These five strata unfold fully in fascicle two's forty-eight minor precepts and the closing five-benefits verse; each stratum takes "sustained contact with the buddhas" as its verbal essence.

This paper treats that architecture and places it as the **sixth layer — grounding-in-precepts** — of the NIAN longitudinal verb-axis. The prior five layers were: P08's *niàn-chēng* four-step coupling (recollection / invocation / remembrance / holding) → P11's *holding the name* (resolute severing paired with steady preservation) → P12's *gathering in the six faculties* (inward spatial integration) → P13's *Universal Gate name-invocation* (three-karmic outward unfolding) → P14's *binding the three periods* (time-segment structuring).[^2] The first five unfold buddha-recollection across the dimensions of verb, cognition, space, three karmas, and time. P15 adds the **precept dimension** — the precept-body as the boundary and temporal extension of the recollection-act, so that buddha-recollection expands from its seated-meditation time-block into a sustained posture across the whole life-world. This is the boundary-completion of P14's time-rhythm structure: P14 answers *when to recollect*; P15 answers *where the boundary of recollection lies*. Together the two papers form the **time-and-space twin axes** of final-age practice.

The argument advances on three levels. **Level one** (§II) is the Indian marginality of recollection in the bodhisattva precepts. Taking Xuanzang's translation of the *Bodhisattva-prātimokṣa* (T1501) as the Indian Yogācāra control — four *pārājayika* plus forty-three minor precepts — we find only **one** clause that ritualizes the recollection of the three jewels: the first minor precept's "at the very least, with a single mind of pure faith, recollect the true qualities of the three jewels." This inward floor (three-fold recollection as daily-minimum threshold) contrasts sharply with T1484's outward peaks (seeing auspicious signs, body-burning offerings, thousand buddhas hand-conferring). Indian bodhisattva precepts center on *intention-pacification*; Chinese bodhisattva precepts center on *contact with the buddha through embodied form*. This contrast frames §III's question: why did the Chinese tradition require such expansion?

**Level two** (§III) is the five-layer structure of sustained recollection in T1484. The paper's core argument. Five decisive clauses from the forty-eight minor precepts — the sixth (thrice-daily reverence), the twenty-third (self-vowed reception with seeing auspicious signs), the thirty-seventh (bimonthly *poṣadha* recitation), the forty-first (offense-atonement through seeing auspicious signs), and the closing five-benefits verse (seeing the thousand buddhas hand-conferring at death) — display how the *Brahma Net* weaves within the precept-architecture a complete action-network of "sustained contact with the buddhas." These five are not independent clauses but five temporal nodes of one and the same "precept-dependent recollection" verb: the daily node (micro-rhythm), the reception node (initiating event), the bimonthly node (collective synchronization), the atonement node (restoration mechanism), and the deathbed node (terminal recovery). Operating together, precept-keeping *is* sustained buddha-recollection.

**Level three** (§IV) is Fazang's ontology of the precept-body. Guifeng Zongmi in P14 displayed the Huayan tradition's **mind-dialectical processing** of final-age recollection — judging subject-object objectifying recollection by the verdict "thinking of the Buddha is also a samsaric mind," yet admitting phenomenal recollection through "bowing to the names of the ten-direction buddhas." Fazang's *Commentary on the Bodhisattva Precept Section of the Brahma Net Sūtra* (T1813) displays the same tradition's **ontological-structural processing** of the same problem. Fazang's six-fascicle commentary contains no independent "buddha-recollection" section; instead, a complete structural chain — precept-body ← Rocana's source ← thousand-hundred-million emanations ← precept-transmission — builds the contact-with-the-buddha mechanism directly into the precept-body itself. Within this structure, precept-keeping *is* sustained alignment with the primordial buddha; the activation of the precept-body *is* the activation of recollection — no "external" name-invocation act need be bolted on. Fazang's commentary-segment on self-vowed reception (glossing T1484's twenty-third minor precept) is the sole passage where the commentary treats a "contact event" directly; its structure stands in counterpoint to Zongmi's phenomenal–noumenal distinction. This constitutes the **third node** of Part V's cross-commentator axis: P12 Jiaoguang (the late-Ming mind-dialectical node on the *Śūraṅgama*) → P14 Zongmi (the mid-Tang mind-dialectical node on the *Perfect Enlightenment*) → P15 Fazang (the early-Tang ontological-structural node on the *Brahma Net*). Viewed together, the three nodes display the full spectrum of Chinese commentarial differentiation on the verb *niàn*: the mind-dialectical school works the **subject of recollection**; the ontological school works the **container of recollection**.

§V positions grounding-in-precepts within the NIAN verb-axis, clarifying its interactions with P11's holding-the-name and P14's time-dependency, and anticipating P16's repentance-based recollection in the *Golden Light Sūtra*. §VI closes with a vertical link to P16.

⚠️ **A functional stance on textual authenticity.** Modern scholarship broadly argues that the *Brahma Net Sūtra* is a mid-fifth-century Chinese composition, not a direct translation from an Indic original; representative studies include Groner (1990), Tsuchihashi Hidetaka (1980), and Funayama Tōru (2013).[^3] This paper does not adjudicate that question. It follows the same methodological stance employed in P12's handling of the *Śūraṅgama* and P14's handling of the *Perfect Enlightenment*: the object of argument is the sūtra's structural role within the Chinese tradition of buddha-recollection practice, while the philological question of Indic provenance is bracketed. Readers interested in the authenticity question should consult the cited works.

## II. The Recollective Margin of the Indian Bodhisattva Precepts

### 2.1 The 4+43 Structure of T1501: Intention at the Center

The *Bodhisattva-prātimokṣa* (T1501), one fascicle translated by Xuanzang, is extracted from the *Śīla-paṭala* (precept chapter) of the *Bodhisattva-bhūmi* of the *Yogācārabhūmi*.[^4] It stands as the rigorous Chinese-translation control for Indian Yogācāra bodhisattva precepts. Its architecture is **four *pārājayika*** (grave defeating offenses) and **forty-three minor precepts**. The four graves are: self-praise and denigration of others, niggardliness of wealth and dharma, anger unassuaged by apology, and slandering the bodhisattva canon while establishing a counterfeit right-dharma. The principle of selection is distinctively Yogācāra: **killing, stealing, sexual misconduct, and false speech are not taken as the bodhisattva's graves.** The rationale is stated in the same text's passage on shared learning with the śrāvakas: "In the remaining precept-items, the bodhisattva should train equally with the śrāvakas, without distinction."[^5] Killing, stealing, sexual misconduct, and false speech have already been handled by the śrāvaka *prātimokṣa*; the bodhisattva observes these in common with the śrāvaka. The bodhisattva precepts do not duplicate the four; they add **four bodhisattva-specific *pārājayika*** — all of them the gravest forms of defiled intention. This structure reflects the Yogācāra presupposition: the bodhisattva precepts are **precepts of the bodhisattva's mind-ground**, addressing the defilements that may still arise after the bodhi-resolve has been aroused, not the basic norms of bodily and verbal action.

The distribution of the forty-three minor precepts exhibits the same presupposition. Comparative analysis shows that roughly sixty percent of T1501's minor precepts are **defilement-pacification clauses** — addressing the five hindrances, pride, anger, sloth, forgetfulness, attachment to service, savoring of meditative absorption, and similar internal afflictions.[^6] This proportion reflects the psychological orientation of Yogācāra bodhisattva discipline: the principal battlefield of the precept is the defilement of mind-consciousness, not external conduct. The contrast with T1484 is total: in T1484's forty-eight minor precepts, analogous defilement-pacification clauses come to less than twenty percent, while the majority are concrete ritual acts (bowing, *poṣadha*, body-burning, eating of meat and the five pungent vegetables and intoxicants, liberation of living beings, and so on). The **psychology-versus-ritual** spectrum is the most conspicuous quantitative index of the difference between Indian and Chinese bodhisattva discipline.

### 2.2 The First Minor Precept — The Lone Ritualization of Three-Fold Recollection

Among the forty-three minor precepts of T1501, the clause most structurally significant for the recollection tradition is **the first minor precept**:

> "Should bodhisattvas abiding in the pure precept-discipline of the bodhisattva, day after day, toward the Tathāgata or the *caitya* raised for the Tathāgata, toward the right dharma or the scripture-scrolls composed for the right dharma — namely the bodhisattva *sūtra-piṭaka* and the *mātṛkā* — or toward the saṅgha, namely the assembly of bodhisattvas of the ten-direction realms who have already entered the great grounds — fail to offer even some small or great offering-item; fail even once, with their body, to bow in reverence; fail even to utter with their voice a single four-line stanza in praise of the true qualities of the three jewels; **fail even, with their mind, to arouse a single pure-faith recollection of the true qualities of the three jewels**; and so pass the day and night empty: this is called an offense, an infraction. It is a defiled infraction."[^7] (T1501, p.1111b; translation mine)

The structure of this clause bears close inspection. The first tier is the **minimum threshold of bodily action** — "at the very least, with one bodily bow" — a single bow as the minimum daily bodily offering. The second tier is the **minimum threshold of verbal action** — "at the very least, with one four-line stanza of praise" — a single stanza as the minimum verbal act. The third tier is the **minimum threshold of mental action** — "at the very least, with a single mind of pure faith, recollect the true qualities of the three jewels" — a single recollection of pure faith as the minimum mental act. The three tiers together compose the complete three-karmic daily-minimum: the lowest guarantee that the day is not spent empty.

The line "at the very least, with a single mind of pure faith, recollect the true qualities of the three jewels" is the **ritualized condensation** of the Indian recollection tradition. *Saddhānussati* (faith-recollection) and the three recollections of buddha, dharma, and saṅgha (*buddhānussati*, *dhammānussati*, *saṅghānussati*) in the Pāli tradition[^8] are here integrated within this Yogācāra precept-book into a single daily-floor clause: each day must arouse at least one "pure-faith recollection of the true qualities of the three jewels," else infraction. This is the **sole** clause in all forty-three of T1501's minor precepts that ritualizes recollection, and the only Indian bodhisattva-precept-text that regulates a recollection-act into a precept.

Yet the very features of this clause also mark the structural ceiling of the Indian recollection tradition. The clause is designed as an **inward minimum threshold**: it legislates only the minimum daily action, setting no ceiling; it handles "not less than this," not "may exceed this"; it establishes a daily floor, not a time-segment peak. Within such a structure, recollection possesses no *saturating time-segment architecture*, no *expectation of the buddha's presence*, no *unification of precept-body with buddha-body* — recollection is simply recollection, one thought suffices, nothing more is anticipated.

The contrast with the *Brahma Net Sūtra* is stark; the next sub-section addresses it.

### 2.3 Why Did the Chinese Tradition Require Structural Expansion?

Within T1484's forty-eight minor precepts, **four** clauses explicitly concern *seeing-buddha correspondence* and *time-rhythm*: the twenty-third (self-vowed reception with seeing auspicious signs), the thirty-seventh (bimonthly *poṣadha*), the forty-first (offense-atonement with seeing auspicious signs), and the closing five-benefits verse (deathbed seeing the thousand buddhas hand-conferring). None of the four finds any parallel in T1501. Indian Yogācāra bodhisattva discipline has no *seeing-signs-for-precept-body* mechanism, no bimonthly *poṣadha* system, no atonement-by-seeing-signs rite, and no final-liturgical five-benefits verse.

Studies in translation history argue that T1484's structural innovation was not "translated" from an Indic original but represents a mid-fifth-century (ca. 431–481) **domestic Chinese reconstruction** of the bodhisattva-precept tradition.[^9] The impetus for this reconstruction can be explained by the difference in field-of-use. T1501 is set within the Indian monastic saṅgha: the premise is that the precept-receiver has already received the śrāvaka *prātimokṣa* (so the four basic grave acts are handled), is ordained as *bhikṣu* or *bhikṣuṇī*, and practices within the monastic institution. T1484 extends the precept-receiver's range to **kings, princes, ministers, commoners, eunuchs, slaves, the eight classes of spirits, animals, even conjured beings** — "as long as they understand the dharma-master's language, all obtain the precepts."[^10] This expansion of the receiver's range means the bodhisattva precepts must answer two demands simultaneously:

**First, the need for basic conduct-norms among lay receivers.** Laypeople have no śrāvaka *prātimokṣa* as foundation; if the bodhisattva precepts do not themselves contain killing, stealing, sexual misconduct, and false speech, the receiver will lack basic behavioral regulation. T1484 places the four at the head of its grave precepts. This is not a structural error but a structural adjustment to the field-of-use.

**Second, the need for contact under the buddha's absence in the degenerate age.** The Indian bodhisattva precepts presuppose a field in which the buddha dwells or a lineage of masters can be reached. Reception has a master, repentance has a master — transmission risks breakage but has institutional guarantees. Yet in the Chinese degenerate-age field — with the Buddha long extinct, the sages and worthies hidden, and perhaps no qualified master within a thousand *li* — without some new mechanism, the receiver can never enter the precept-body at all. T1484's twenty-third minor precept accordingly designs a **backup path of self-vowed reception**: seven days of repentance before buddha-and-bodhisattva images; the seeing of an auspicious sign confers the precept. The auspicious sign becomes the **verification mechanism of precept-body formation under the buddha's absence** — through correspondence, direct contact with the absent buddha.

Under these two demands, the precepts can no longer be mere intention-pacification (Indian Yogācāra's center); they must be, simultaneously, **a sustained form of contact with the buddhas**. Every precept-keeping act must carry the possibility of correspondence — thrice-daily reverence can correspond with the dharma-master's buddha-status (the sixth minor precept); bimonthly *poṣadha* can be held before buddha-and-bodhisattva images (the thirty-seventh); atonement-repentance can correspond with the Buddha coming to rub the crown, with light, with flowers (the forty-first); at death, with the thousand buddhas hand-conferring (the five-benefits verse); self-vowed reception can correspond with an auspicious sign appearing (the twenty-third). The precepts are not a collection of norms but a **sustained action-network for activating the buddhas' correspondence**.

This is the Chinese answer to what we might call the *recollective-margin problem* of the Indian bodhisattva precepts — not to enlarge the precept-clauses but to recast the precept-body. The precept-body itself becomes the container of contact with the buddhas — a container activated simultaneously at the five nodes traced in §III.

## III. The Five-Layer Structure of Sustained Buddha-Recollection in the *Brahma Net*

Within the ten major and forty-eight minor precepts of fascicle two, five decisive nodes together compose the complete action-network of **precept-dependent recollection**. These five are not independent clauses but five temporal dimensions of one and the same act — the micro-rhythm (daily), the initiating event (reception), the collective synchronization (bimonthly), the restoration mechanism (atonement), and the terminal recovery (deathbed). The five operate on distinct time-scales: hourly for the daily node, two-weekly for the bimonthly node, event-bounded (intensive retreats of up to a year) for the reception and atonement nodes, and lifetime-bounded for the deathbed node. Operating together, the precept-body remains in continuous contact with the buddhas at every temporal unit. The five constitute §III.1–5 below.

### 3.1 The Reverence Layer — Thrice-Daily Micro-Rhythm (The Sixth Minor Precept)

The sixth minor precept handles the bodhisattva's duty of reverence toward a Mahāyāna dharma-master, serving as the **micro-rhythmic base** of the five-layer structure:[^11]

> "If a buddha-son sees a Mahāyāna dharma-master, or a Mahāyāna fellow-student of the same view and practice, entering a monastic compound or a city — whether having traveled a hundred *li* or a thousand — one should rise to meet and send them off, bow, and make offerings. Thrice daily one should make offerings — daily offering three ounces of gold, a hundred flavors of food, bedding, seat, and medicine; serve the dharma-master in all that is needed. One should constantly invite the dharma-master to expound the dharma thrice daily, **thrice daily offer bows**, without arousing a mind of anger or vexation, giving up one's body for the dharma, requesting the dharma without slackening. Otherwise, one commits an offense of light defilement." (T1484, fasc. 2, p.1005b22–c01; translation mine)

Three features structure this precept. **First, the rhythm-setting:** "thrice daily" — morning, midday, and evening — the smallest time-rhythm built into the precept-body. This tri-temporal structure is the classical source of the Chinese monastic pattern of morning-and-evening services plus midday offering; it is not a later patriarchal invention but a daily rhythm the receiver must install from the precept-level itself. **Second, the object's nature:** the object of reverence is the "Mahāyāna dharma-master" — the dharma-master's physical presence is the manifestation of the Buddha's speech; the preceptor's body is the concrete form of the three jewels. The master is not the *possessor* of the precept-body but its *transmitter*; reverence to the master is reverence to the precept-body, which is reverence to Rocana as the precept-body's source. **Third, action-continuity:** "rise to meet, see them off, bow, make offerings" — the full time-span from the master's arrival to departure is the field of reverence, not a one-time act at formal offering alone.

The contrast with T1501's first minor precept ("at the very least, with a single mind of pure faith, recollect the true qualities of the three jewels") is instructive. Both set a "thrice-daily" rhythm, but T1501's requirement is an **inward minimum** (one thought of faith-recollection suffices), whereas T1484's is an **outward three-karmic** requirement (bodily bow + verbal request-for-dharma + mental absence of irritation). T1484 expands "the inward one thought" of the Indian precept into "outward three karmas" — each of the three daily intervals is not merely a moment of recollection but a complete three-karmic reverence-event.

The structural significance: the reverence layer bears the task of **daily sustained contact with the precept-body**. Without this layer, the other four (reception, bimonthly, atonement, deathbed) have no everyday foundation. Reception is a one-time event, the bimonthly a fortnightly occurrence, atonement triggered by offense, the deathbed once in a life — all are non-quotidian peaks. Only the reverence layer supplies the daily base-rhythm. If the precept-body is not activated by daily reverence, its high-point activations will have no supporting substrate. The reverence layer is therefore the **micro-rhythmic base** of the five.

### 3.2 The Reception Layer — Self-Vowed Seeing-Signs as the Initiating Event (The Twenty-Third Minor Precept)

The twenty-third minor precept is the single clause in the entire *Brahma Net Sūtra* that intersects most deeply with NIAN's concerns. It prescribes how, after the Buddha's extinction and where no ordination-master can be reached within a thousand *li*, the precept-receiver completes precept-body formation:[^12]

> "If a buddha-son, after the Buddha's extinction, wishing with a good mind to receive the bodhisattva precepts, stands before buddha-and-bodhisattva images and self-vows to receive them, **then for seven days before the Buddha one should repent; obtaining an auspicious sign, one obtains the precepts**. If no auspicious sign is obtained, one continues for two-sevens, three-sevens, and up to a year — an auspicious sign must be obtained. Having obtained the auspicious sign, one then receives the precepts before the buddha-and-bodhisattva images. If no auspicious sign is obtained, though one receives before the buddha-image, one does not obtain the precepts. If one has previously received the bodhisattva precepts in the presence of a dharma-master, no auspicious sign is required. Why? Because the dharma-master has been transmitted master-to-master. For this reason, receiving before the dharma-master is obtaining the precept — for one arouses the gravest mind, one obtains the precept. **If within a thousand *li* there is no qualified ordination-master, one may receive before the buddha-and-bodhisattva images — but the auspicious sign is required.**" (T1484, fasc. 2, p.1006c05–12; translation mine)

Within this single clause the *Brahma Net* establishes the **most basic divergence** between Chinese and Indian bodhisattva-precept systems. T1501's reception system is **master-to-master transmission**: the receiver must perform the *karma*-act before a *pudgala* (at least one to three witnesses); the precept-body is established under the master's witness. Where no master can be reached, pure intention and self-vowing may substitute, but such self-vowing is a **temporary expedient**; when a master later becomes available, formal reception is still required. T1484 opens another path: self-vowed reception is not a temporary expedient but a **legitimate route on par with master-transmission**, its condition being the *seeing of the auspicious sign*.

The structural significance of "seeing the auspicious sign" is weighty. On one hand, it replaces the Indian mechanism of *master-witnessing* — the master's absence is replaced by the buddha's present correspondence. On the other, it defines a new species of religious event: the **direct contact-event between the receiver and the absent buddhas**. This contact is not metaphorical and not merely psychological — T1484 explicitly states: "**the auspicious signs are: the Buddha coming to rub the crown, seeing light, seeing flowers, and various extraordinary signs.**"[^13] The Buddha, though extinct, though not in this Sahā world, though a thousand *li* or more distant, is still able to manifest in concrete form after seven days' repentance, making contact with the precept-body.

The time-structure — "seven days, two-sevens, three-sevens, up to a year" — is tightly designed. At minimum seven days, at maximum one year, one must see an auspicious sign to obtain the precepts. If after a year none is seen, the precept-body is not established. The window acknowledges that contact is not arbitrarily summoned; it requires cumulative intensive investment. It also acknowledges that some receivers may never complete the contact (those who fail within a year) — their precept-body cannot be formed by the self-vowed route; such receivers must still rely on a master. The threshold of reception is not lowered; it is reconfigured — the self-vowed route opens for those capable of correspondence; those not yet open to it must still rely on a preceptor.

Another structural feature is the **visibility requirement**: the receiver must actively enter a state in which contact can occur. The seven-day repentance is not passive waiting but active clearing of karmic obstruction so the precept-body's correspondence-window can open. The repentance-act includes bowing, precept-recitation, name-invocation, image-contemplation, and vow-making — while "seeing the auspicious sign" serves as the judgment-criterion, the *process* is itself a complete set of recollection-acts. The receiver performs this set continuously across the seven days until correspondence manifests. This is the concentrated embodiment, within the reception-event, of **precept-keeping and recollection operating as equal-weight dual axes**.

This precept is the **precept-level root** of the subsequent Chinese tradition of "seeing-buddha correspondence." Shandao's deathbed vision in the *Commentary on the Contemplation Sūtra* in Four Fascicles, Tiantai's seeing-buddha practice within the four samādhis, the *chán*-*jìng* dual-cultivation's enlightenment-vision of the Buddha, the divination-sign of the *Sūtra on the Divination of the Effect of Good and Evil Actions* — all inherit the structural DNA of this clause. It is not that these later traditions *imported* seeing-buddha elements from outside; rather, the precept-body design itself already built in the seeing-buddha correspondence mechanism, and the later traditions unfolded their distinct directions from that design.

The twenty-third minor precept is therefore the **initiating event** of the five-layer structure — reception is the precept-body's beginning, the auspicious sign is the beginning's completion-criterion, and that completion is essentially the precept-disciplinary form of buddha-recollection samādhi.

### 3.3 The Recitation Layer — Bimonthly *Poṣadha* as Collective Synchronization (The Thirty-Seventh Minor Precept)

The thirty-seventh minor precept treats the four pillars of bodhisattva conduct: the two-season *dhūta*, the eighteen essential items, the bimonthly *poṣadha*, and the rainy-season retreat. Among these, the bimonthly *poṣadha* passage is the core of this layer:[^14]

> "On the *poṣadha* day, the new-learner bodhisattva, fortnight by fortnight, recites the ten major and forty-eight minor precepts. **At the time, before the images of all buddhas and bodhisattvas, one person conducts the *poṣadha* — one person alone recites**. Whether there be two, three, or up to a hundred thousand, still only one recites. The reciter takes the high seat; the listeners sit below. Each wears the nine-stripe, seven-stripe, or five-stripe robe." (T1484, fasc. 2, p.1007c23–1008a08; translation mine)

The clause establishes a **fortnightly collective precept-recitation rhythm**. Bimonthly *poṣadha* is an ancient institution of the śrāvaka *vinaya* — reciting the *prātimokṣa* on the eighth, fifteenth, twenty-third, and thirtieth (or twenty-ninth) of the month is the saṅgha's standard rite of self-purification. The *Brahma Net* transposes the institution into the bodhisattva-precept system, with three key adjustments.

**First, the field-adjustment:** *poṣadha* must be held "before the images of all buddhas and bodhisattvas." The śrāvaka *poṣadha*'s traditional field is the precept-platform, the dharma-expounding hall, or any clean place of saṅgha assembly; the *Brahma Net* explicitly requires recitation before buddha-and-bodhisattva images. The character of the *poṣadha* shifts from **the saṅgha's internal self-purification** to **the precept-body's renewal before the buddha**. The images are not the background of the *poṣadha* but its co-presence — each recitation is an event of "recitation before the buddha."

**Second, the sonic architecture:** "one person conducts the *poṣadha* — one person alone recites; the reciter takes the high seat; the listeners sit below." This sonic architecture is foundational to Chinese collective practice — one voice emits, the many receive; the reciter sits high, listeners below. It is structurally isomorphic with the later Pure Land design of "leading-the-assembly name-invocation" with sound upon sound unbroken — one voice emits, the many dwell within the sound; the subject of the sound is not the individual mind-stream of the reciter but **the precept-body itself reciting before the buddhas**. The architecture expands individual recollection into **collective recollection**: at the bimonthly *poṣadha*, the activation of the precept-body is not a single person's event but a resonance-event of the whole saṅgha before the buddhas.

**Third, the eighteen-items configuration:** the bodhisattva on *dhūta* must carry eighteen items, including **buddha-and-bodhisattva images**:[^15]

> "Willow twig and soap-bean; the three robes, bottle, bowl, sitting-cloth, and staff; incense-burner and water-strainer; hand-towel, knife; fire-striker and tweezers; rope-bed, scriptures and *vinaya*; **buddha-and-bodhisattva images**. When bodhisattvas walk the *dhūta*, or wander on journeys a hundred or a thousand *li*, these eighteen items ever follow the body." (T1484, fasc. 2, p.1007c27–1008a02; translation mine)

Images rank with robes, bowl, and staff among the *dhūta*'s essentials — the *materialization* of recollection. The image functions as the extension of the ascetic's body; wherever the bodhisattva walks, the buddha is present. Walking a thousand *li*, with no fixed dwelling, the precept-body's contact with the buddhas is never broken, because the image walks with the body — *poṣadha* may be held before the image along any route.

This precept is the **collective-synchronization node** of the five-layer structure: fortnightly as rhythm, recitation as act, "before the buddha" as field, the eighteen items as vehicle. The four elements together re-activate the precept-body across the community at a two-week scale.

### 3.4 The Repentance Layer — Atonement with Seeing Auspicious Signs as Restoration Mechanism (The Forty-First Minor Precept)

The forty-first minor precept prescribes the path of redress for violators of the ten grave precepts; its architecture forms a **mirror-counterpart** to the twenty-third:[^16]

> "If there is one who has violated the ten precepts, one should teach them to repent. **Before the images of all buddhas and bodhisattvas, day and night across the six periods, one should recite the ten major and forty-eight minor precepts. Bowing to the exhaustion of one's strength to the three-period thousand buddhas, one should obtain the auspicious sign**, whether in a seven-day period, two-or-three-sevens, up to a year — the auspicious sign must be seen. **The auspicious sign is: the Buddha coming to rub the crown, seeing light, seeing flowers, and various extraordinary signs — then the offense is extinguished.** If no auspicious sign arises, though one repents, it is of no avail." (T1484, fasc. 2, p.1008c12–19; translation mine)

The mirror-structure with the twenty-third is apparent. Compare the corresponding elements:

| Element | Twenty-third (reception) | Forty-first (atonement) |
|---------|--------------------------|-------------------------|
| Event | Self-vowed precept-reception | Repentance for violation |
| Field | Before buddha-and-bodhisattva images | Before buddha-and-bodhisattva images |
| Action | Seven days of repentance before the Buddha | Day-and-night six-period recitation + exhaustive bowing to the three-period thousand buddhas |
| Time-window | Seven days, two-sevens, three-sevens, up to a year | One-seven, two-or-three-sevens, up to a year |
| Completion-criterion | Obtaining the auspicious sign = obtaining the precepts | Obtaining the auspicious sign = extinguishing the offense |
| Failure-outcome | Without the sign, no precept obtained | Without the sign, repentance is vain |

The two precepts share the same structural skeleton — **before the image, through cumulative recollection-action, through seeing the auspicious sign, the event is completed**. They differ only in event-type: reception is the positive establishment of the precept-body, atonement its negative restoration. That the same skeleton operates in both signifies that the *Brahma Net* possesses a unified action-paradigm for "contact-with-the-buddha events": **beginning at seven days, extending to a year, before the image sustaining recollection-action until the Buddha's crown-rubbing or the seeing of light and flowers occurs**. It is the initiating mechanism of reception and the restoring mechanism of violation alike; establishment and restoration use the same action-structure.

The significance of this mirror-structure: the *Brahma Net*'s precept-body is **not a static asset built once and unchanged** but a **dynamic process of ongoing possible damage and ongoing possible restoration**. Positive establishment and negative restoration belong to the same life-cycle of the precept-body; the same seeing-the-sign correspondence threads through both poles. Precept-keeping, under this structure, expands from an initiating event into a **sustained action** — each possible violation is the trigger for precept-body restoration; each restoration is the re-activation of recollective correspondence. Precept-keeping is therefore not "not-violating-after-receiving" but **"sustaining the capacity for contact with the buddhas within the precept-body's life-cycle."**

This precept is the prototype of the later Chinese repentance liturgies. The divination-signs of the *Sūtra on Divination*, Samantabhadra's manifestation in the *Sūtra on the Practice of Samantabhadra*, the bowing-repentance-and-seeing-buddha of the *Vaipulya-samādhi Ritual*, the great-compassion repentance's sincerity-correspondence — all take **seeing the auspicious sign** as the criterion of efficacy. The scriptural ground of that criterion is the forty-first minor precept: the efficacy of repentance depends neither on the count of recitations, nor on the number of bows, nor on the repenter's self-report, but on whether the Buddha manifests with crown-rubbing, light, or flowers in witness. The criterion is severe: it rules out self-consoling repentance (however sincere the repenter feels, absent the sign, the offense is not extinguished) and formalized repentance (however many recitations, absent the sign, of no avail).

The forty-first minor precept is thus the **restoration-mechanism node** of the five-layer structure — the restoration of violation is the renewed summoning of recollective correspondence; the completion of atonement is the re-establishment of contact between precept-body and buddha.

### 3.5 The Safeguarding Layer — Deathbed Seeing the Thousand Buddhas as Terminal Recovery (The Closing Five-Benefits Verse)

The closing passage of fascicle two contains two crucial texts: the general charge on precept-recitation's transmission, and the verse of the five benefits of precept-keeping. Together they form the **terminal-recovery node** of the five-layer structure:[^17]

> "All you buddha-sons, listen well! These ten major and forty-eight minor precepts — **the buddhas of the three times have recited, shall recite, and now recite**; I now also thus recite. All you great assemblies — kings, princes, ministers, *bhikṣu* and *bhikṣuṇī*, faithful men and women — who have received and kept the bodhisattva precepts, should receive, keep, read, recite, expound, and write the scroll of the ever-abiding buddha-nature precepts, transmitting through the three times so that all sentient beings transform-after-transform unbroken. **They shall see the thousand buddhas, each buddha hand-conferring; life after life not falling into evil paths or the eight inopportune conditions, ever reborn in human realms or the heavens.**"

> "When one of wise endurance is strong, / able to keep such a dharma: / before reaching buddhahood, / five benefits are peacefully obtained: / **first, the buddhas of the ten directions, / with sympathetic recollection, constantly guard him**; / **second, at the moment of death, / right view and joyful mind**; / third, wherever one is born, / one is friend to bodhisattvas; / fourth, the accumulation of merit, / the precept-perfection is fully accomplished; / fifth, in this life and the next, / nature-precept, merit-and-wisdom are full." (T1484, fasc. 2, pp.1009b29–c04 and c21–25; translation mine)

The **general-charge passage** is structurally important for its **three-time character of precept-keeping**. "The buddhas of the three times have recited, shall recite, and now recite" — the subject of precept-keeping is never a specific individual in a specific space-time, but **the co-recitation of the buddhas of the three times**. The precept that the receiver recites at the bimonthly *poṣadha*, or in daily precept-keeping, is the same precept recited by the three-time buddhas. The reciter's identity is re-positioned within the great commonwealth of "three-time buddhas." The structural effect is decisive: the precept-keeper, in every precept-act, resonates with the three-time buddhas — precept-keeping is not individual religious practice but membership in the **three-time buddhas' precept-keeping commonwealth**. Under this membership, the precept-keeper is no longer a solitary individual facing a set of precept-clauses, but a node in a three-time resonance network.

The **five-benefits verse** carries the structural meaning of the precept's correspondence-cashing-in. The first and second benefits — "the buddhas of the ten directions, with sympathetic recollection, constantly guard him" and "at the moment of death, right view and joyful mind" — constitute the prototype of the Chinese tradition of **deathbed welcoming by the buddhas**.

**First benefit:** "the buddhas of the ten directions, with sympathetic recollection, constantly guard him." The precept-keeper, in this life, abides in the condition of "being continuously recollected by the buddhas." "Sympathetic recollection" is not occasional regard but **constant guardianship** — ceaseless, moment-by-moment recollection-and-holding. Under this structure, **precept-keeping is being-recollected-by-the-buddhas**. The distinction between precept-keeper and buddha-recollector dissolves here — the precept-keeper is the recollector, only the active parties differ: the precept-keeper's act is precept-keeping; the buddhas' act is sympathetic recollection. The correspondence-circuit is bidirectional: the act of precept-keeping activates the buddhas' recollection-and-holding; the buddhas' recollection-and-holding stabilizes the precept-body. This is the final form, within the five-layer structure, of the thesis that **precept-keeping is buddha-recollection**.

**Second benefit:** "at the moment of death, right view and joyful mind." At the instant of death, the precept-keeper's mind is not perverted; right view stands forth; joy is present. This is structurally isomorphic with the *Contemplation Sūtra*'s lowest-grade-lowest-birth passage "Amitābha manifests before him," Shandao's "right mindfulness at the moment of death," and the *Amitābha Sūtra*'s "at death the mind is not perverted." The *Brahma Net*'s five-benefits verse precedes Shandao by roughly a century and a half, and precedes the systematic integration of the three Pure Land sūtras — it may be regarded as a **precept-level predecessor** of the Pure Land discourse on deathbed right-mindfulness.

The phrase "they shall see the thousand buddhas, each buddha hand-conferring" is the most concrete figurative description of this terminal recovery. At the moment of death or in a future rebirth, the precept-keeper sees the thousand buddhas manifest, and is hand-conferred by buddha upon buddha in sequence — structurally parallel with Amitābha's nineteenth vow ("at the moment of death, should I not appear before him surrounded by the great assembly, may I not attain perfect enlightenment"), the welcoming passages of the *Contemplation Sūtra*'s various grades, and the simultaneous manifestation of many buddhas in the *pratyutpanna-samādhi*. The reduplication "buddha by buddha" carries particular structural density: not one buddha alone but **the thousand buddhas, one by one, manifesting and hand-conferring**. Each buddha's hand-conferring is a confirmation of the precept-body, a formal admission of the precept-keeper into the commonwealth of the buddhas.

The five-benefits verse thus elevates the reward of precept-keeping from "future-life merit" to the **final convergence with all buddhas**. Precept-keeping's meaning lies not in worldly benefit in this or future lives — it is membership in the commonwealth of the three-time buddhas, a terminal guarantee of the thousand buddhas' hand-conferring at death. Precept-keeping is recollection; recollection is precept-keeping — the two merge completely within the closing five-benefits verse and can no longer be separated.

The closing verse is therefore the **terminal-recovery node** of the five-layer structure — a lifetime's precept-keeping is cashed in at death as the thousand buddhas' hand-conferring; the life of the precept-body is fully sealed in closure.

### 3.6 Integrating the Five Layers

The five-layer structure is now complete. The five are not isolated clauses but five temporal dimensions of one "precept-dependent recollection" verb — daily (micro-rhythm), reception (initiating event), bimonthly (collective synchronization), atonement (restoration mechanism), deathbed (terminal recovery). They operate on distinct scales but share one underlying structure: **the precept-body is activated before the buddha-image, cultivated through cumulative action, and completed or cashed in by the seeing of auspicious signs**.

Within the *Brahma Net* the five are not scattered clauses but a coherent **life-system of the precept-body**. Through this system, the receiver's precept-body develops from the initiating event (reception) into daily rhythm (reverence), synchronizes collectively at the bimonthly cycle (recitation), is restored in case of violation (atonement), and finally is cashed in at death as the welcoming of the buddhas (safeguarding). Operating together, the precept-body remains in continuous contact with the buddhas across the receiver's whole religious life — this is the complete action-structure of "precept-keeping as sustained buddha-recollection."

The contrast with T1501's forty-three minor precepts is decisive: T1501 has the daily-recollection floor (the first minor precept) but none of the other four layers; T1484 has all five. The **structural expansion of Chinese bodhisattva discipline** is not a simple enlargement of the Indian prototype but a structural reorganization of "recollection as the daily floor" — an expansion from an **inward minimum threshold** into an **inward-and-outward five-layer network**.

The impetus has been argued above: the need for basic conduct-norms among lay receivers plus the need for contact under the Buddha's degenerate-age absence. Together, these two pressures compel the reorganization — precepts cannot be intention-pacification alone; they must simultaneously be sustained contact with the buddhas. Chinese bodhisattva discipline, beyond the Indian tradition, independently develops a complete action-grammar of **precept-dependent recollection**.

## IV. Fazang's Ontology of the Precept-Body — "The Precept-Body Is the Primordial Buddha"

Fazang (643–712), third patriarch of Huayan, systematized the school's dharma-realm vision and nature-arising doctrine through his *Huayan Wujiao zhang* and *Huayan-jing tanxuan-ji*.[^18] His *Commentary on the Bodhisattva Precept Section of the Brahma Net Sūtra*, in six fascicles, is the Tang Huayan tradition's authoritative commentary on T1484.[^19] A striking structural feature for NIAN's purposes: **Fazang's entire commentary has no independent "buddha-recollection" section.** The term *niànfó* 念佛 appears in the six fascicles only in marginal idioms — "recollecting the goodness of buddha-nature" (citing the *Nirvāṇa Sūtra*), "remembering the primordial practice of the buddhas" (citing the *Avataṃsaka*), "recollecting kindness with reverence" (glossing filial piety). None of these functions as an independent practice-gate.

This absence should not be underestimated. Unlike Zongmi in P14, who through "thinking-of-the-Buddha is also a samsaric mind" and "bowing to the names of the ten-direction buddhas" constructs a dual-layer verb of phenomenal and noumenal recollection, Fazang does not walk this path at all in the *Brahma Net* commentary. He does not, however, evade the problem of contact-with-the-buddha; he handles it differently — **ontological-structural processing** as against Zongmi's **mind-dialectical processing**.

This differentiation forms the third node of Part V's cross-commentarial axis. P12's Jiaoguang Zhenjian, in his commentary on the *Śūraṅgama*, distinguishes "faculty-gathered recollection" from "recollection by the discriminative mind" — late-Ming *Lengyan* study in the mind-dialectical school. P14's Zongmi, in his *Subcommentary* on the *Perfect Enlightenment*, establishes phenomenal versus noumenal recollection — mid-Tang *Yuanjue* study in the same school. P15's Fazang, in his *Brahma Net* commentary, sets up the structural chain of precept-body ← Rocana's source ← emanation-transmission — early-Tang Huayan study in the **ontological school**. Viewed together, the three nodes display the full spectrum of Chinese commentarial differentiation on recollection: Jiaoguang and Zongmi dismantle subject-object from the **subject** side; Fazang builds the ontological container of **what is recollected**. The two wings are not opposed but complementary — the mind-dialectical school handles "how to recollect"; the ontological school handles "what the recollected buddha is." Only the two wings viewed together yield the full Chinese commentarial picture of buddha-recollection.

Four sub-sections unfold Fazang's ontological processing: §4.1 the source-ontology (thousand-flower, thousand-hundred-million Rocana as original body); §4.2 the three-fold negation of precept-essence; §4.3 the self-vowed reception's seeing-signs correspondence (the commentary's sole direct handling of a "contact event"); §4.4 the counterpoint with Zongmi.

### 4.1 Thousand-Flower Thousand-Hundred-Million — Fazang's Source-Ontology

In the opening of the commentary's preface, Fazang lays down the structural skeleton of the whole work:[^20]

> "On the thousand flowers, the thousand-hundred-million: Rocana is the original body; the ten major and forty-eight minor: Śākyamuni-Mañjuśīra is the terminal emanation. … Moreover, precept-items numbering as the Ganges sands — the three-pure-aggregates perfectly gathered by conditions; dust-number stern clauses — the six positions fully encompassed by conditions. Like Indra's net, same yet not-same; like the *sarvajña* ocean, different yet not-different. … Therefore the bodhisattvas of the five positions, none but rely on this as cause-perfection; the tathāgatas of the three times, none but rely on this as fruit-perfection." (T1813, fasc. 1, p.602b01–10; translation mine)

The preface locks the *Brahma Net*'s discipline into the Huayan source–emanation architecture: **Rocana is the original body** (primordial buddha, source, ontological ground); the **thousand-hundred-million Śākyamunis are the terminal emanations** — Śākyamuni as Rocana's manifestation, the ten major and forty-eight minor precepts as the dharma transmitted by the emanation. The precepts do not originate from any particular new promulgation by Rocana or Śākyamuni at some specific time; they are "law-so-of-the-dharma-realm" (*fǎjiè fǎ'ěr* 法界法爾), the intrinsically pre-existing. Fazang makes this explicit: "The original precepts of the buddhas of the three times are law-so-of-the-dharma-realm, not newly instituted. The Rocana bodhisattva practiced these precepts, perfected the positions, attained buddhahood, and in the Lotus-Treasury World, desiring to benefit all beings, himself recited them out, transmitted them to the emanations, who accord with each being's capacity in Jambudvīpa expediently reciting and manifesting. Thus do we possess such a precept-book."[^21] The transmission is vertical-tripartite: **law-so-of-the-dharma-realm → Rocana's self-recitation → emanation-transmission to beings**. Three verbs — *self-recitation, transmission, reciting-and-manifesting* — compose the vertical recollective-preservation chain of the precepts.

The significance for NIAN: Fazang does not install "buddha-recollection" as an independent practice, yet the source–emanation architecture itself is ontologically a kind of "buddha-recollection" — every recitation and every keeping-of-the-precept is an activation of this vertical chain. Beings' recitation joins Śākyamuni's transmission; Śākyamuni's transmission joins Rocana's self-recitation; Rocana's self-recitation joins the dharma-realm's law-so-original precepts. Under this architecture, **precept-keeping is a structural act of holding-in-memory the buddhas' source** — no "external" name-invocation is needed. Recollection is not established as an independent practice; it exists as the substratum structure of precept-body operation within every precept-keeping act.

Fazang introduces two Huayan metaphors that further specify this operation: **Indra's net** and the ***sarvajña* ocean**.[^22] "Like Indra's net, same yet not-same; like the *sarvajña* ocean, different yet not-different." Indra's net is Śakra's jeweled net, each pearl reflecting every other pearl in layered infinity — each node is simultaneously itself, reflector of the others, and reflected-in-the-others-reflecting-others without end. The *sarvajña* ocean is the "ocean of omniscience," all species of wisdom held together, sameness manifest within difference. Applied to the precepts, the effect is: every precept-receiver is a node in Indra's net; within this receiver's precept-body are reflected the precept-bodies of the thousand-hundred-million Śākyamunis on the thousand flowers; within the thousand Śākyamunis' precept-bodies are reflected this receiver's precept-body; the precept-body's establishment is not a solitary individual event but **the arising of the entire network of buddhas' precept-bodies at the node of this receiver**. "Same yet not-same" — all precept-bodies belong to one precept, yet each node reflects in its own way; "different yet not-different" — the nodal differences do not obstruct essential identity.

Fazang further lists five hermeneutic readings (*五說判釋*) of the relation between Rocana and the thousand-hundred-million Śākyamunis.[^23] His judged-for-the-sūtra position is the **third reading**: "Within the Lotus-Treasury World, seated on the flower-dais, a true-body attains buddhahood, named Rocana. Above the thousand flowers are the thousand Śākyamunis — one Śākyamuni in each of the thousand Maheśvara heavens; each of these thousand Śākyamunis manifests as a hundred million Śākyamunis. Thus there are thousand-hundred-million Śākyamunis attaining buddhahood simultaneously beneath the bodhi-trees of a thousand-hundred-million realms." This is a three-level structure — **primordial / retribution / emanation**: Rocana as original body (one true-body attaining buddhahood), the thousand Śākyamunis on the thousand flowers as retribution-body, the hundred-million emanations as emanation-body. Yet Fazang's deeper position is his **fifth reading**: "According to the *Avataṃsaka*, there is neither attainment nor non-attainment, so Rocana in all places attains buddhahood as true-body. Moreover, Rocana *is* Śākyamuni, without the two-position distinction of retribution and emanation — only the ten bodies are spoken, so as to display the inexhaustible; thus the field of conversion is boundless and endless. … In every dust within the flower-treasury world, all worlds manifest as Indra's net, thus layered without end, beyond all conceiving — and these are all places where Rocana ever turns the dharma-wheel."

This fifth reading is the pivot. Under the third reading, recollection still has a definite object — the recollected buddha is Rocana on the flower-dais or the emanation-Śākyamuni of Jambudvīpa, a specific other. Under the fifth reading, **the recollected buddha is no longer some specific other at some location but the ontological essence pervading everywhere** — within every dust is Rocana's wheel-turning place; the receiver's precept-body, activated before the buddha-image, is in that very moment a nodal manifestation of Rocana at this place. This shift in the personhood of the recollected buddha utterly reconstructs the structure of recollection: recollection is not a mental act directed at an external object but a **structural holding-in-memory, at the precept-body's locus, of the self-awareness that this locus is Rocana**.

Fazang provides a further ontological scaffolding in his gloss on "the teaching-host" — the **two meanings of Vairocana**: "The Sanskrit scripture reads *Vairocana*, rendered here 'light-pervading-illumination.' Yet there are two meanings: first, inwardly, with the light of wisdom illumining the true dharma-realm — this is the meaning of **self-enjoyment**; second, outwardly, with the light of body illumining the great capacities — this is the meaning of **other-enjoyment**."[^24] Self-enjoyment is Rocana's ontological self-authentication — the inner wisdom-light illumines his own true dharma-realm; other-enjoyment is Rocana's responsive manifestation — the outer bodily-light responds to beings' capacities. For the recollection-structure this has weight: the Rocana reached in beings' recollection is neither pure self-enjoyment (which is Rocana's inner self-authentication, unrelated to beings) nor pure other-enjoyment (which would be Rocana's one-way beneficent impartation); rather, **at the activation-point of the precept-body, the ontological light of self-enjoyment simultaneously unfolds itself in the form of other-enjoyment toward beings**. The ontological light and its responsive manifestation converge at the node of the precept-body. The practitioner's precept-body is at once the receptacle of the other-enjoyment light and a nodal point of the self-enjoyment light here; precept-keeping is "participating, at this node, in Rocana's joint self-and-other-enjoyment luminosity."

Fazang's source-ontology may now be integrated: **the precept-body is not the inner property of some individual practitioner but the contact, at this practitioner's body, of the vertical recollective-preservation chain dharma-realm → Rocana → emanation → beings; recollection is not this practitioner's subjective mental act but the objective event of this vertical chain being activated by the precept-body.** Precept-keeping is holding-in-memory; holding-in-memory is recollection — the three converge completely under Fazang's source-ontology.

### 4.2 The Three-Fold Negation of Precept-Essence — Apart from Form-and-Mind, Being-and-Non-Being, Cause-and-Effect

Fazang's source-ontology provides the **external structure** of the precept-body — its position in the cosmos, its relation to Rocana. The **internal structure** is set forth in the commentary's "On the Profound Precept-Body" section, the densest ontological passage in the whole commentary, defining the precept-body's essence through three "apart-from" negations:[^25]

> "Second, on the profound precept-body. … Of three clauses: first, **apart from form-and-mind**, meaning this true precept-nature is not of resistant quality, neither is it discursive apprehension, hence 'neither form nor mind.' Further: since the precept is established upon the seed-of-thought, using the thought-seed as essence, it is said to be 'not blue and the other forms'; but on the thought-seed it is only provisionally posited as form, hence 'not mind.' Second, **apart from being-and-non-being**: the conditionally-arisen precept has no self-mark; being-the-condition is not the precept; apart from the condition, no precept; excluding both identity and separation, no middle ground obtains. Thus sought, the precept is permanently neither being nor non-being. … Further: because it is not the grasped, it is not being; because it arises from conditions, it is not non-being. Again, because it arises from conditions, it is not being; because it is not the grasped, it is not non-being — hence 'neither being nor non-being.' Third, **apart from cause-and-effect**: the nature-conformed precept leaves behind both producer and produced, hence it is neither cause nor effect. … Hence in absolute nature, it is neither cause nor effect." (T1813, fasc. 1, pp.607c22–608a10; translation mine)

The three negations are not three independent judgments but three complementary faces of one and the same precept-body. **First, apart from form-and-mind**: the precept-body is neither *rūpa* (resistant quality as the defining feature of form) nor *citta* (discursive apprehension as the defining function of mind). The negation has two concrete targets. One is the Sarvāstivāda **non-informative-form theory of precept-essence** (*avijñapti-rūpa*) — on which view, the precept-essence is a special "non-informative form" evoked by the informative acts of body and speech at the moment of reception, persisting uninterruptedly in the recipient's continuum until it is relinquished or until death. Fazang negates: if precept-essence were form, it would possess resistance, be quantifiable, be perceivable — but the essence of precept-essence is light, light-pervasive, light-upon-light inexhaustible; no resistant form can sustain it. The other is the Yogācāra **thought-seed theory of precept-essence** — for which precept-essence is the seed of the thought-mental-factor, planted in the *ālaya-vijñāna* at reception, the thought-seed serving as the precept-essence's self-body. Fazang takes a subtle stance: first affirming "the precept is established upon the thought-seed, using the thought-seed as essence" (accepting, at the conditionally-arisen level, that the precept-body genuinely takes the thought-seed as the basis of its arising); then immediately adding "on the thought-seed it is only provisionally posited as form — hence 'not mind'" (the precept-body is not directly identical with the thought-seed's essence but is a structural existence provisionally set up on the thought-seed, whose essence transcends the mental-factor-property of the thought-seed).

**Second, apart from being-and-non-being.** The passage unfolds a Huayan-Madhyamaka tetralemma on the precept-body. (1) *If the precept is the condition* — if it equals the sum of causal conditions (reception-rite, preceptor, precept-clauses) — the precept is that collection, not the precept itself. (2) *If the precept is apart from the condition*, independent of all causal conditions, there is no verifiable mode of manifestation, no real subsistence. (3) Excluding both identity and separation. (4) No middle obtains between them. By this reasoning, the precept-body does not fall into the category of "being" (no independently verifiable precept-body exists). Yet "because it is not the grasped, it is not being; because it arises from conditions, it is not non-being" — nor does it fall into "non-being": though not graspable as real, its conditionally-arisen functioning is genuine; though genuinely functioning, it is not graspable as real. The precept-body is therefore **"neither being nor non-being"** — transcending the two extremes while subsisting as conditional functioning. The argument is structurally isomorphic with Nāgārjuna's eightfold negation, applied to the ontological positioning of the precept-body.

**Third, apart from cause-and-effect.** "The nature-conformed precept leaves behind both producer and produced, hence it is neither cause nor effect." The precept-body is neither productive cause (of some future reward) nor produced effect (of some past good). The target is the understanding of "the precept-body as a link in the karmic causal architecture." Were the precept-body a cause (generating future blessings) or an effect (produced by past good karma), it would be locked into the producer-produced frame of karma. Fazang negates: the precept-body is **nature-conformed** (*chèn-xìng* 稱性) — directly identical with dharma-nature — and dharma-nature is itself neither cause nor effect, transcending the producer-produced. The precept-body, being one with dharma-nature, is likewise neither cause nor effect. The structural meaning: the precept-body is not a link in the karmic causal architecture but **an ontological existence transcending karmic causality**; precept-keeping's meaning lies neither in "obtaining future blessings" nor in "settling past karmic debt" but in **direct contact with dharma-nature**.

After the three negations, Fazang immediately turns to "On the Broadness of the Precept's Function," unfolding the structure of "though not effect, it can act as effect; though not cause, it can act as cause":[^26]

> "'The foundation of the buddhas of the three times' — below, on the broadness of the precept's function. Of three: first, though the precept is not effect, it can act as effect, hence 'the foundation of the buddhas'; second, though the precept is not cause, it can act as cause, hence 'the root of bodhisattvas.'" (T1813, fasc. 1, p.608a10–12; translation mine)

This is the typical Huayan structure of **simultaneous obstruction-and-illumination** (*zhē zhào tóng shí* 遮照同時). The three negations are *obstruction* — excising false grasping upon the precept-body; the dual affirmations are *illumination* — revealing the precept-body's positive function. The two levels are not sequential but simultaneous: the precept-body's "is-not" is the ontological condition of its "can-act"; being neither form nor mind, it can serve as the common foundation of buddhas; being neither being nor non-being, it can function equally in all beings; being neither cause nor effect, it can stand its own function outside the karmic frame.

The bearing of this ontology on the NIAN verb-grammar can be stated thus: **the precept-body is the ontological pre-condition of Huayan-style "possibility of recollection."** If the precept-body were form (Sarvāstivāda), the ontological basis of recollection would be restricted to the arising of a certain class of form; if the precept-body were thought-seed (Yogācāra), the ontological basis of recollection would be restricted to the activation of a mental-seed; both lock recollection's possibility into a limited ontological category. Fazang's three negations elevate the precept-body to **the status of nature-conformed ontology** — the precept-body is dharma-nature's nodal arising at the body of this practitioner. Upon this ontological basis, precept-keeping *is* holding-in-memory the buddhas' source; the activation of the precept-body *is* the initiation of recollection — no "external" name-invocation need be bolted on.

An interpretive tension with §III deserves clarification here. §III developed, on the basis of the sūtra-text itself, the five-layer structure of thrice-daily reverence, bimonthly recitation, self-vowed seeing-signs, bowing to the three-period buddhas for atonement, and deathbed thousand-buddha welcoming — with the twenty-third minor precept's "seven-day repentance invoking the names of buddhas and bodhisattvas" and the forty-first's "exhaustive bowing to the three-period thousand buddhas" clearly involving concrete name-invocation and reverence acts. Fazang's commentary does not deny these acts, nor does it reinterpret them as "non-recollection." The structural effect of the commentary is not to cancel the concrete acts but to **provide them an ontological basis** — under Fazang's ontological architecture, concrete invocation and reverence are no longer understood as "the objectifying recollection of an ordinary mind" (the pathology Zongmi warns against), nor as "instrumental acts for acquiring merit" (a folk-religious misreading), but are repositioned as **concrete manifestations of the precept-body's operation at the phenomenal surface** — invocation is one mode of the precept-body's functioning; reverence is another; all these surface acts are the ontological arising of the precept-body at a specific space-time. This is Fazang's **ontological re-reading** of the *Brahma Net*'s sūtra text.

### 4.3 The Seeing-Signs Correspondence of Self-Vowed Reception — The Commentary's Sole "Contact-with-the-Buddha Event"

Fazang's ontological-structural processing is thoroughgoing — no passage in the six fascicles describes a concrete "seeing-the-buddha" or "corresponding-to-the-buddha" act as a daily practice guide. Yet one exception stands: in glossing the twenty-third minor precept (self-vowed reception), Fazang directly treats a concrete "contact-with-the-buddha event." This is the commentary's sole "seeing-buddha passage," functionally exceptional within Fazang's structure:[^27]

> "In the earlier part, again three: first, self-vowed reception; second, reception from a master; third, explanation of the self-vowed intent. Within the first, again three: first, the initial time of reception — when the Buddha is present, self-vowed reception is not used, hence 'after the extinction.' 'Wishing-to' is the mind of sincerely seeking the precepts; 'good mind' is the bodhi-mind of pure faith in accord with principle. These two serve as the precept-vessel, hence they are raised. … The signs that should be taught are of three kinds: **one, the mind expressed as sign** — 'if my mind is false and I truly cannot practice the bodhisattva's practices, may the buddhas and bodhisattvas not give me the precepts; if truly they will give them, since I know my mind is true, I thereby know I have obtained the precepts.' **Two, cool winds arising from the various directions** — hence one knows the precepts are obtained. **Three, beginning with 'should be seven days' below, explaining the conditions for obtaining the precept**: first do the seven-day dharma, that is, enter a clean ritual site and repent offenses obstructing the precept. **'Auspicious sign' — the Buddha coming to rub the crown and so forth, just as the sūtra says.** For those with heavy obstruction, up to a year; though a year passes, if no auspicious sign is obtained, the precepts are in the end not obtained. … Self-vowing is not so — self-recitation of the *karma*, oneself having no precept: from whom shall it be obtained? It must be from the Buddha or the bodhisattvas. Since the place of buddhas and bodhisattvas is hard to know, one must express it through the auspicious sign; only then are the precepts obtained — hence the sign is required." (T1813, fasc. 5, p.645a19–b12; translation mine)

Fazang unfolds a three-tier structure of "signs that should be taught." The three are not independent options but three nodes along the same contact event's strength-spectrum.

**First tier: the mind expressed as sign.** "If my mind is false and I truly cannot practice the bodhisattva's practices, may the buddhas and bodhisattvas not give me the precepts; if truly they will give them, since I know my mind is true, I thereby know I have obtained the precepts." This is the receiver's inner self-verification, with the truth or falsity of one's own mind as the first criterion. Before the self-vow, the receiver raises a conditional petition: if my mind is false and unable to practice the bodhisattva's practices, let the buddhas and bodhisattvas withhold the precepts; if it is true, let them grant. The structural subtlety is exquisite: it does not presuppose that the receiver can directly verify the truth or falsity of his own mind (the deepest difficulty of self-knowledge); rather, the buddhas' and bodhisattvas' granting or withholding becomes the objective criterion. If the precept-body is in fact in contact, the receiver "since I know my mind is true, I thereby know I have obtained the precepts"; if not, the receiver knows his mind is false. **The buddhas' and bodhisattvas' verdict serves as the mirror in which the mind is seen** — this is the first tier.

**Second tier: cool winds arising from the various directions.** "Cool winds arising from the various directions — hence one knows the precepts are obtained." This is the environment's intermediary sign. Should the receiver's precept-body genuinely make contact during the self-vowing, subtle changes in the external environment (cool winds from the four directions) serve as the sign of that contact. The structural significance: the precept-body's contact is not a purely inner event of the receiver but simultaneously involves the receiver's external environment — the precept-body's establishment elicits a subtle adjustment in physical space-time; cool winds rise naturally in the four quarters as a physical projection of the precept-body's source-light outward-radiating. This tier extends the contact-event from a purely psychological occurrence into a **psycho-physical resonance** — the precept-body's source-light radiates into the environment upon contact; the environment responds with subtle change.

**Third tier: the Buddha coming to rub the crown.** "'Auspicious sign' — the Buddha coming to rub the crown and so forth, just as the sūtra says." This is the strongest outward manifestation — the Buddha himself appearing, verifying the precept-body through bodily contact. Under the highest intensity, the contact-event triggers the Buddha's manifestation and touch. Crown-rubbing is concrete bodily contact, the most direct form of precept-transmission; the Buddha's light, flowers, and various extraordinary signs (just as the sūtra says) all express this strongest contact.

The three tiers in concert compose a **strength-spectrum from inner to outer** — from inner self-verification (mind as sign), through environmental intermediary (cool winds), to bodily manifestation (crown-rubbing). The three need not all appear; the receiver may obtain confirmation at any tier. Nor need they appear sequentially; they are three nodes of different contact-strength, with different manifestations according to the receiver's capacity and obstruction.

Fazang's deepest reasoning in this passage concerns **why the auspicious sign is necessary**: "Self-vowing is not so — self-recitation of the *karma*, oneself having no precept: from whom shall it be obtained? It must be from the Buddha or the bodhisattvas. Since the place of buddhas and bodhisattvas is hard to know, one must express it through the auspicious sign; only then are the precepts obtained — hence the sign is required." The logic is tight: (1) the self-vowing receiver has no precept — the purpose of reception is to obtain one; (2) the precept-body must be obtained from some source — it cannot be the receiver himself (who has no precept), so it must be the Buddha or bodhisattvas; (3) contact with the Buddha or bodhisattvas is not directly knowable by the receiver — "the place of buddhas and bodhisattvas is hard to know"; (4) an **objectively verifiable mark** is therefore required — this is the auspicious sign; (5) without it, the precept-body's contact cannot be confirmed and the precept-body is not established — "though a year passes, if no auspicious sign is obtained, the precepts are in the end not obtained."

The structural effect is crucial. **The auspicious sign is not ornamental to religious experience but an ontological necessity for the precept-body's establishment.** Its necessity lies not in satisfying the receiver's subjective expectation ("let me feel the buddha's presence") but in constituting the objective channel of precept-transmission ("let the buddhas' and bodhisattvas' precept-body truly reach me"). Under this structure, seeing-signs correspondence belongs not to the category of "mystical experience" but to the category of **ontological verification** — it is the precept-body's contact-mark, as a thermometer reading is a mark of temperature.

A further important face of this effect: **the contact-event is confined to the special moment of the precept-body's establishment; daily precept-keeping inherits and cashes in that contact**. The seven days (or up to a year) of self-vowed reception is a special span within which the contact-event must occur; once it has occurred and the precept-body is established, every subsequent daily precept-act is **inheriting that precept-body** and **cashing in that contact**. Daily precept-keeping does not require the re-staging of contact-events every day — that would transform precept-keeping into an endless chase after correspondences and destroy the precept-body's stability. Fazang's architecture exhibits its subtlety here: the contact-event is a **one-time ontological establishment event**; daily precept-keeping is a **sustained cashing-in action** — their division of labor is clear and mutually supporting.

The integration with §III is straightforward. §III exhibited the twenty-third minor precept's seven-day repentance and seeing-signs mechanism at the level of the sūtra-text — the **sūtra's prescription**. Fazang's commentary supplies the ontological necessity of the prescription — why it must be so, why without it nothing. **Sūtra-prescription and commentary-interpretation support each other**: the sūtra provides the concrete act; the commentary provides the ontological ground. The sūtra tells the receiver what to do; the commentary tells him why to do so. Viewed together, the overall architecture of the *Brahma Net*'s self-vowed-reception mechanism is presented — **at once a precept-disciplinary mechanism (sūtra) and an ontological event (commentary), establishing a lifelong precept-body through a one-time contact**.

This is the most concrete unfolding of Fazang's ontological-structural processing: no independent recollection chapter, yet the contact-with-the-buddha is built directly into the precept-body's establishment event. The next sub-section contrasts this ontological structure with Zongmi's mind-dialectical structure.

### 4.4 Ontological-Structural versus Mind-Dialectical — The Two Wings of the Huayan Commentarial Legacy

P14 and P15 together display the two paths by which Huayan's commentarial tradition handled the problem of recollection. Guifeng Zongmi (780–841) in the *Subcommentary on the Abridged Commentary to the Sūtra of Perfect Enlightenment* proceeds by **mind-dialectical processing** — "thinking of the Buddha is also a samsaric mind" warns against subject-object objectifying recollection; "bowing to the names of the ten-direction buddhas" admits phenomenal recollection as a final-age entry; the four diseases (fabrication, passivity, cessation, annihilation) serve as the metronome of recollective practice. Fazang (643–712) in the *Commentary on the Bodhisattva Precept Section of the Brahma Net Sūtra* proceeds by **ontological-structural processing** — the source–emanation–transmission chain lays the ontological pre-conditions of recollection; the three-fold negation demarcates the precept-body's ontological status; the self-vowed-reception seeing-signs correspondence supplies the event-mechanism of contact. Though both belong to the Huayan legacy, their processing dimensions diverge entirely.

The correspondences across six dimensions can be tabulated:

| Dimension | Zongmi (P14, mind-dialectical) | Fazang (P15, ontological-structural) |
|-----------|-------------------------------|-------------------------------------|
| **Diagnosis** | Thinking-of-the-Buddha is also a samsaric mind — subject-object objectifying recollection easily collapses into deluded mind | The precept-body *is* the primordial buddha — the container of recollection is Rocana's source; the buddha recollected pervades everywhere |
| **Remedy** | Phenomenal recollection (admissible: bowing to ten-direction names) / noumenal recollection (breaking subject-object, returning to object-less recollection) | Seeing-signs correspondence — a one-time contact at the precept-body's establishment, cashed in lifelong |
| **Time-structure** | Every present moment of recollection — awake with each arising, pure with each awakening; every instant is the occasion for correction | Precept-body established once, cashed in lifelong — the contact-event is confined to a special moment; daily precept-keeping inherits it |
| **Processing dimension** | Subject of recollection — addressing the action-grammar of "how not to fall into subject-object" | Container of recollection — addressing the ontological question of "what the recollected buddha is" |
| **Concrete mechanism** | Four-disease metronome — fabrication / passivity / cessation / annihilation, four modes of alert | Three-fold negation + source-ontology — apart from form-and-mind / apart from being-and-non-being / apart from cause-and-effect + Rocana – thousand-hundred-million – emanation chain |
| **Object-correspondent** | Concrete emanation — the buddha named in invocation or bowing is a recognizable buddha-name | Ontological structure — Rocana pervades everywhere; every dust, every world, is a node of true-body buddhahood |

The structural divergences carry three levels of implication.

**First, the two schools' processing dimensions do not overlap.** Zongmi asks: when a final-age practitioner in a specific space-time arouses the act of recollection, does that act itself fall into the pit of samsaric delusion? This is the **action-grammar** question — about the internal structure of this single recollection-act, the presence or absence of subject-object, the moment's truth or delusion. Fazang asks: when a receiver in a specific space-time arouses the act of precept-keeping, what buddha does this act correspond to; what is the ontological status of the precept-body; how does contact with the buddha come about? This is the **ontological** question — about the status of the recollected object, the ontological properties of the precept-body, and the structural conditions of the contact-event. The two are distinct processing levels — action-grammar concerns the internal nature of this single act; ontology concerns the external architecture of this act.

**Second, the two schools' time-structures are mutually complementary.** Zongmi's time-structure is **instant-by-instant** — every present moment of recollection is an opportunity for cultivation; every instant may fall into or escape the subject-object pit. Its strength is subtlety — the practitioner can remain alert in every instant, with every instant a possible advance. Its weakness is the inability to secure a stable sustained action — if every moment requires alertness, cultivation becomes an endless self-check and easily succumbs to recursive doubt. Fazang's time-structure is **event-bounded** — the precept-body is established once at a special time (the seven days of self-vowed reception), and daily precept-keeping is thereafter a stable cashing-in. Its strength is stability — the receiver, after the precept-body's establishment, does not need to re-stage contact daily and can unfold daily practice on the stable basis of the precept-body. Its weakness is coarseness — it does not handle each instant's micro action-grammar, nor guard against possible subtle losses in daily practice. Viewed together, **Zongmi supplies Fazang's subtlety at the instant; Fazang supplies Zongmi's stability at the event** — the two time-structures support each other.

**Third, the two schools together constitute the complete lineage of Chinese commentarial recollection-grammar.** Part V's three nodes at P12, P14, P15 display three commentators' handling of "recollection": Jiaoguang Zhenjian (late-Ming *Lengyan* study), Guifeng Zongmi (mid-Tang *Yuanjue* study), and Fazang (early-Tang Huayan study). Jiaoguang and Zongmi belong to the **mind-dialectical school** — both dismantle the subject-object structure of recollection, processing from the side of mind-dialectic. Fazang belongs to the **ontological school** — building the ontological architecture of what is recollected, processing from the side of the precept-body's ontology. Viewed together, the three display the complete spectrum of Chinese commentarial differentiation on recollection: **the mind-dialectical school handles "how to recollect"; the ontological school handles "what the recollected buddha is."**

The three nodes also display a historical dimension of differentiation. Fazang (643–712, early Tang) processes ontologically within the peak flourishing of Huayan scholastic discourse; Zongmi (780–841, mid-Tang) processes mind-dialectically within the consolidation phase of Huayan; Jiaoguang (1547–1627, late Ming) processes the *Lengyan* mind-dialectically within a period of sectarian convergence. The ontological processing was completed at the very founding of Tang Huayan scholastic discourse; thereafter, successive commentators on different sūtras and treatises repeatedly developed the mind-dialectical line. The historical trajectory suggests: the ontological school was established early; the mind-dialectical school grew dominant in later centuries. Yet within this sūtra's handling, the two should be viewed **in parallel, not by selection** — each is complete in itself, each has its own processing dimension; the overall structure of Chinese recollection-thought requires both wings in concert to be displayed.

Fazang's ontological architecture in the *Brahma Net* commentary thus becomes NIAN's third node: after P12 (the late-Ming mind-dialectical summation on the *Śūraṅgama*) and P14 (the mid-Tang mind-dialectical exemplar on the *Perfect Enlightenment*), Huayan's ontological processing supplies the ontological foundation for the container-level of the recollection problem. The next section situates this foundation within the NIAN verb-axis, clarifying its structural position as the sixth layer — grounding-in-precepts.

## V. The Verb-Grammar Position of the Dual Axis — Grounding-in-Precepts as NIAN's Sixth Layer

This section situates P15's precept-architecture within the NIAN longitudinal verb-axis. The previous five layers (P08–P14) were reviewed in §I; here the sixth layer's internal position is clarified, along with its interactions with prior layers and its structural preparation for P16.

**The content of "grounding-in-precepts"**: the sustained activation of the recollection-act across the whole life-span, through the precept-architecture as its boundary and temporal extension. The prior five layers primarily treated the unfolding of recollection **within the seated-meditation time-block** — the *niàn-chēng* four-step coupling (P08), holding-the-name's severing-and-preserving (P11), the spatial inward integration of gathering the six faculties (P12), the three-karmic outward unfolding (P13), and the time-segment structuring of "binding the three periods" (P14). The processing object of the five layers was, broadly, "the recollection-act within the seated time-block." P15's grounding-in-precepts addresses **how the recollection-act continues outside the seated time-block** — when the practitioner leaves the meditation-field and enters daily life's time-segment, how does recollection not break, how is the precept-body continuously activated? Answer: by grounding-in-precepts. The precept-clauses are the recollection-boundaries of daily-life time — not-killing, not-stealing, not-sexual-misconduct, not-false-speech, not-intoxicants, and the basic precepts demarcate, at the daily level, "the time-space range within which recollection may remain continuously activated." Every act of upholding a precept is an activation of the precept-body — which is the arising of recollection.

**Interaction with P11 (holding-the-name).** P11 argues that holding-the-name is "severing plus preserving" — severing the stray thought, preserving the name. The structure operates well within the seated block; P15's grounding-in-precepts **temporalizes** the structure — holding-the-name is no longer the action of some single seated block but extends across the whole life-span. The precept-body is the temporal container of holding: the stable boundary supplied by the precept-architecture allows preservation of the name to remain unbroken across daily comings and goings; the normativity supplied by the precept-architecture gives the severing of stray thought a concrete criterion in daily incidents. Holding-the-name thus expands from an **intra-seated act** to a **whole-span sustained act** — and the mechanism of expansion is grounding-in-precepts.

**Interaction with P12 (gathering the six faculties).** P12 argues that gathering the six faculties is spatial integration — the six faculties (eye, ear, nose, tongue, body, mind) converging inward upon the recollected buddha within the seated block. In daily-life time, however, the faculties must be open outward — the eye must see, the ear must hear, the body must act, the mind must respond. The inward convergence of the seated block cannot be held. The precept-architecture provides the **daily-time faculty-management mechanism**: not-killing, not-stealing, not-sexual-misconduct are **bodily-faculty norms in daily time**; not-false-speech, not-divisive-speech, not-harsh-speech, not-frivolous-speech are **verbal-faculty norms**; not-intoxicants, not-eating-the-five-pungents are **gustatory-faculty norms**; not-eating-meat is the **mental-faculty norm (compassion) in daily time**; not-watching-perverse-song-dance, not-wearing-floral-garlands, not-sitting-on-high-broad-seats are **norms of eye-faculty, ear-faculty, and body-faculty**. Each precept-clause is a daily-receptive norm of one faculty — the precept-architecture *is* the **operational regulation of the six faculties in daily time**, the daily-form of gathering-the-six-faculties. P12's inward convergence within the seat and P15's institutional management in daily life operate at different time-segments; together they compose the six faculties' sustained reception across the whole span.

**Interaction with P14 (binding the three periods).** P14 answers *when to recollect* — the question of time-rhythm (the morning, midday, evening tri-temporal structure). P15 answers *where the boundary of recollection lies* — the question of action-boundary (the whole-action system of precept-norms). Together, the two papers form the **time-and-space twin axes of final-age practice**:

- **Time axis (P14)**: morning / midday / evening — three temporal nodes for concentrated recollection.
- **Space/action axis (P15)**: the whole-action system within the precept-boundary — every act of daily time unfolds within the precept-boundary; the maintenance of that boundary is the sustained activation of recollection.
- **The two axes in orthogonal combination**: grounding-in-precepts operates sustainedly within each time-segment; grounding-in-time regulates the rhythm within each act. The time axis's rhythm and the space axis's boundary together compose the complete practice-architecture of the final-age practitioner.

This twin-axis structure has its concrete embodiment in the Chinese monastic tradition: morning-and-evening services at the three times (P14's concrete realization) together with daily precept-norms across comings and goings (P15's concrete realization) compose the complete practice-time-space of the saṅgha. For the lay practitioner: simplified thrice-daily reverence at waking, noon-offering, and bedtime (P14's lay adjustment) together with precept-keeping in daily work and life (P15's lay form) compose the complete practice-time-space of the householder. The interaction of the two axes is not a theoretical abstraction but the actual operational structure of Chinese practice.

**Foreshadowing P16, the *Golden Light Sūtra***. P15's precept-architecture will combine with P16's repentance-architecture to compose the middle-run of Part V.

- **P15 (precept-architecture)**: the precept-body's establishment and maintenance — reception as initiating event; daily reverence as micro-rhythm; bimonthly *poṣadha* as collective synchronization; self-vowed seeing-signs as the formation mechanism.
- **P16 (repentance-architecture)**: the precept-body's restoration and renewal — post-violation repentance; post-atonement seeing of auspicious signs; dream repentance at the golden drum's sound; Bodhisattva Ruciraketu's dream-manifestation; Elder Jalavāhana's son rescuing the fish and performing repentance; giving one's body to the starving tigress as body-life repentance.

The structural relation: **P15 handles the precept-body's positive face (establishment) and stable face (maintenance); P16 handles its negative face (violation) and restorative face (repentance)**. They mirror each other — establishment and restoration use the same "seeing auspicious sign" correspondence (the mirror-structure already laid down in §3.4); maintenance and renewal use the same "sustained recollection" verbal structure. On the NIAN longitudinal axis, P15's grounding-in-precepts and P16's grounding-in-repentance together compose the **precept-and-repentance twin axes** — precept-keeping as positive dimension, repentance as negative-restorative dimension; operating together, the precept-body preserves a sustained dynamic balance across the whole religious life.

This twin axis will be completed in Part V's middle run: P13 (three-karmic outward unfolding in the Universal Gate) → P14 (time-segment rhythm in the *Perfect Enlightenment*) → P15 (precept-boundary action-system in the *Brahma Net*) → P16 (repentance-architecture restoration in the *Golden Light*). Only at the fourth node will Part V's middle-run Chinese practice-skeleton be fully displayed.

## VI. Conclusion — Precept-Keeping Is Sustained Recollection; the Precept-Body Is the Container of the Primordial Buddha

This paper has argued on three levels. **Level one** contrasted T1501's Indian Yogācāra bodhisattva precepts with T1484's Chinese *Brahma Net* precepts, revealing the structural expansion of the Chinese tradition: from the inward floor of T1501's "at the very least, with a single mind of pure faith, recollect the true qualities of the three jewels, thrice daily," to T1484's five-layer outward peaks of thrice-daily reverence + bimonthly recitation + self-vowed seeing-signs + atonement seeing-signs + deathbed thousand-buddha hand-conferring. The impetus is the need for basic conduct-norms among lay receivers plus the need for contact under the Buddha's absence in the degenerate age — in the Chinese setting, bodhisattva discipline is recast from *intention-pacification* into *the sustained form of contact with the buddhas*. **Level two** unfolded the internal operation of the *Brahma Net*'s five-layer structure: reverence as micro-rhythmic base; reception as initiating event; recitation as collective synchronization; atonement as restoration mechanism; safeguarding as terminal recovery. Five layers on distinct time-scales, sharing one underlying structure — the precept-body activated before the buddha-image, cultivated through cumulative action, completed or cashed in through auspicious signs. **Level three** interpreted the structure through Fazang's ontological-structural processing: source-ontology (thousand-flower thousand-hundred-million Rocana as original body); three-fold negation (apart from form-and-mind / apart from being-and-non-being / apart from cause-and-effect); self-vowed seeing-signs correspondence (the commentary's sole "contact-with-the-buddha event"). The three pillars together compose the ontological-school path that **the precept-body is the primordial buddha**; joined with Zongmi's mind-dialectical processing from P14, they constitute the two-wing differentiation of Huayan commentarial processing on Part V's cross-commentator axis.

The structural meaning of **grounding-in-precepts** as NIAN's sixth layer is thereby established: the recollection-act expands from seated meditation to daily life, from a specific act to a whole-action system. Precept-keeping is sustained recollection; the precept-body is the container of the primordial buddha. This is not a metaphorical statement but a structural proposition — in the *Brahma Net*'s precept-body design, the precept-body indeed operates as a "sustained container of contact with the buddhas"; in Fazang's ontological interpretation, it indeed subsists as "the nodal arising of Rocana's source at the practitioner's body." Viewed together, the structural status of the precept-body is the manifest status of the primordial buddha; the act of precept-keeping is the act of recollection.

Four concrete suggestions may be distilled from this structural analysis for the contemporary reader's practice.

**First, reverence as the minimal action-unit.** The sixth minor precept's "thrice-daily reverence" is the lowest threshold of precept-body activation — no extended meditation, no elaborate ritual, no sectarian qualification; only thrice-daily, one bow. In daily householder life this is: one bow at waking (morning), one bow before the midday offering (midday), one bow at bedtime (evening) — three bows per day, under one minute of time invested, is enough to activate the micro-rhythmic base. Of the *Brahma Net*'s five layers, this is the easiest to land, and is the daily entry to the entire architecture.

**Second, small-scale daily recitation as a substitute for the bimonthly *poṣadha*.** The thirty-seventh minor precept's "bimonthly recitation of the ten major and forty-eight minor precepts" was originally the saṅgha's collective rite — not available to the isolated contemporary householder. One may instead recite the ten grave precepts once at waking and once at bedtime each day (about one minute each), thereby fulfilling the bimonthly *poṣadha*'s functional substitute: in the twice-daily recitation-act, the precept-body is re-affirmed before the buddha-image, and the precept-boundary is re-activated in consciousness. Small-scale daily recitation structurally takes on the bimonthly's function of "periodic renewal of the precept-body," compensating for the absence of collective synchronization by higher frequency.

**Third, the reception is not a past event but the continuously activated precept-body of the present.** Many receivers treat reception as the past event of some day in some year — once the certificate is obtained, the precept-body is regarded as a "possessed" static asset. The *Brahma Net*'s precept-body structure is the opposite — the precept-body is a **dynamic container requiring sustained activation**, not a possessed static asset. Every act of reverence, recitation, and precept-keeping is a present activation; without the present act, though the precept-body was established at past reception, it lies dormant in the present. The real meaning of reception is therefore not the acquisition of some religious identity but **opening a precept-body channel to be sustainedly activated across a whole life** — the channel's subsistence depends on sustained use; disuse gradually closes it.

**Fourth, precept-keeping is being-recollected-by-the-buddhas.** The first of the five benefits — "the buddhas of the ten directions, with sympathetic recollection, constantly guard him" — is the ontological return of precept-keeping. The precept-keeper, in this life, abides in the condition of being continuously recollected by the buddhas. This is not an abstract religious assurance but a structural state: every act of precept-keeping activates the precept-body; the activation of the precept-body is contact with the buddhas' source; the contact is the sustained event of being-recollected-by-the-buddhas. **The distinction between precept-keeper and recollector dissolves here** — precept-keeping is recollection, recollection is precept-keeping, the two merging completely in the precept-body's activation. The practitioner in daily precept-keeping need not install a separate "recollection" act — every precept-act is already a recollection-act; every precept-moment is a moment of being-recollected-by-the-buddhas. This is the deepest practical directive of the *Brahma Net*'s precept-body design for daily practice: **precept-keeping itself is being-recollected.**

**Vertical foreshadowing of P16, the *Golden Light Sūtra***. The *Golden Light Sūtra* (T0663 Dharmakṣema trans. / T0664 Paramārtha trans. / T0665 Yijing trans.) supplies four key structures as the canonical ground of Part V's middle-run mirror of repentance: first, **the dream-golden-drum repentance** — Bodhisattva Ruciraketu sees in a dream a great brahmin striking a golden drum with a mallet, the drum emitting a subtle voice speaking the buddhas' merits and repentance verses; on waking, Ruciraketu recites before the Buddha what he heard in the dream — dream as the triggering structure of repentance; second, **Ruciraketu's dream-manifestation** — his body in the dream turning gold, buddhas in the dream revealing the essentials of repentance — dream as a type of buddha–practitioner contact, mirroring P15's self-vowed seeing-signs through "seven-day repentance"; third, **Jalavāhana's son rescuing the fish** — the elder's son seeing ten thousand fish dying in a drying pool, transporting water on elephants to save them and then preaching the twelve-linked dependent arising and the buddhas' names for the fish — the typical three-karmic synthetic repentance structure; fourth, **giving one's body to the starving tigress** — in a past life as Prince Mahāsattva, Śākyamuni sees seven tigers dying of hunger and offers his body-life as alms, repenting the karmic obstruction of all beings — the ultimate form of body-life repentance. The four in concert complete Part V's middle-run precept-and-repentance twin axis — P15's precept-architecture (the precept-body's positive establishment) and P16's repentance-architecture (the precept-body's negative restoration) compose the complete dynamic balance of Chinese practice. P16 will unfold, on this basis, a structural argument that **repentance is recollection, repentance is precept-body restoration, repentance is the renewed summoning of the buddhas' sympathetic recollection**.

P15's argument here concludes. The sixth layer, grounding-in-precepts, has established its structural status on the NIAN longitudinal axis; the *Brahma Net*'s precept-body design and Fazang's ontological interpretation together compose the complete action-grammar of "precept-keeping as sustained recollection"; Part V's cross-commentator axis reaches its third node in this chapter; Part V's middle-run precept-and-repentance twin axis will be completed in P16, the *Golden Light*.

[^26]: T1813, fasc. 1, "On the Broadness of the Precept's Function" section, T40n1813, p.608a10–12; translation mine (quoted in §4.2). The typical Huayan structure of simultaneous obstruction-and-illumination: three negations (*obstruction*) followed by two affirmations ("though-not-yet-able-to-act" — *illumination*).

[^27]: T1813, fasc. 5, glossing the "Precept on Deceptive Teaching for Profit," "Self-vowed / Master-received" section, T40n1813, p.645a19–b12; translation mine (quoted in §4.3). This is the only passage in the entire commentary that directly treats a "practitioner–buddha contact event." Fazang constructs the strength-spectrum of the contact-event through three kinds of "signs that should be taught" (mind-as-sign / cool winds / Buddha rubbing the crown), and supplies the ontological-necessity argument for the auspicious sign through "since the place of buddhas and bodhisattvas is hard to know, it must be expressed through the auspicious sign."

### IV. Cross-Reference to Series Papers

- **P08** *From Recollection to Name-Invocation*: the *niàn-chēng* four-step coupling — the verbal-ontology starting point of recollection / invocation / remembrance / holding. P15's grounding-in-precepts layer assumes that four-step coupling has been established and unfolds the sixth dimension on the precept-architecture.
- **P11** *Holding the Name*: severing and preserving as the parallel name-act — the intra-seated internal structure of holding. P15 §V discusses how holding is temporalized through grounding-in-precepts, extending from the seated block to the whole life-span.
- **P12** *Gathering in the Six Sense-Roots*: spatial inward integration — the dual-axis intra-seated structure of faculty-reception. P15 §V discusses how gathering-the-six-faculties is institutionalized through grounding-in-precepts, taking the precept-clauses as the six-faculty operational norms in daily time.
- **P13** *Universal-Gate Name-Invocation*: three-karmic outward unfolding — the three-karmic architecture of Lotus buddha-recollection. P15 with P13 composes the outward dimension of final-age practice.
- **P14** *Binding the Three Periods*: time-dependent verbal grammar of final-age recollection — time-rhythm (the morning-midday-evening tri-temporal structure); Zongmi's dual-layer recollection (phenomenal/noumenal) and the four-disease metronome; the Zongmi-versus-Fazang pairing as the first counterpoint of the Huayan commentarial mind-dialectical / ontological-structural dual lineage. P15 §IV directly answers P14; §V discusses how the two papers together compose the time-and-space twin axes of final-age practice.
- **P16** *Golden Light Sūtra*: repentance-based recollection — the four structures of the dream-golden-drum, Ruciraketu's dream-manifestation, Jalavāhana's son rescuing the fish, and giving one's body to the starving tigress; with P15 composing Part V's middle-run precept-and-repentance twin axis.
