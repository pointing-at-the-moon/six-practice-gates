# PRAṆIDHĀNA: Aspiration and the Vow
### From Pāli Roots to Samantabhadra

The English edition of **《願——從巴利誓願到普賢行願》** by 釋慧鏡 (Shi Huijing)—Volume 6, the final volume, of the **Six Practice Gates** (六行門).

> A wish asks outward for a thing; a vow turns the whole person's direction around. This book follows that turning from the spread of hair before Dīpaṃkara, through the wholesome desire of the Āgamas, the driver behind the ox of merit, the four great vows in everyone's mouth, the forty-eight clauses that burned into a land, and Samantabhadra's conduct-vows—into the deep stretch where the vow meets emptiness, and out at the Heart Sūtra's last word: *attain*.

This is the accessible *book* layer, rendered into English from the Chinese edition and term-anchored to the bilingual scholarly corpus in this repository (`pranidhana/papers/`, P01–P20). It lives here at `pranidhana/book/en/`, a part-for-part parallel of the Chinese book under `pranidhana/book/`.

## The road

Eight parts, one direction:

**Pāli Roots · The Vow in the Āgamas · The Vow as Perfection · The Four Great Vows · The Original Vows of Amitābha · Samantabhadra's Conduct-Vows · The Vow and Emptiness · Attaining Bodhi**

One direction, turned and held: from five flowers and a spread of hair, up the ladder of "may I…", onto the curriculum of the bodhisattva path, into four sentences that were the Four Noble Truths all along, through a vow that became a Buddha and a land, into the single word "conduct-vow"—then face to face with emptiness, past the gate of nirvāṇa without entering, and on to the one unbroken breath in which the buddhas of the three periods attain anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi. The final chapter turns over the bottom of the whole series: what the six gates—recollection, giving, repentance, discipline, concentration, vow—have been, all along.

## How this edition is made

Each chapter is translated from the approved Chinese chapter and term-anchored to its source paper, so that every doctrinal term uses the rendering the author has already settled. Sūtra and śāstra quotations reuse the verified English from the papers wherever they exist. Romanizations and quick definitions are gathered in [Appendix C · Glossary](appendix/C_glossary.md); each chapter's scriptural sources are indexed in [Appendix A · Academic Notes Index](appendix/A_academic-notes-index.md).
