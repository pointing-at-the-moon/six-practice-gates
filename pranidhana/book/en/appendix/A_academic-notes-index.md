# Appendix A · Academic Notes Index

The main text was written to be spoken to you. So the catalog numbers, the fascicle references, the source locations—all of these have been ushered out of its pages and gathered here.

This index serves two purposes. First, it lets the reader who wants to look closer go from any chapter of the book back to the exact place in the sūtras and treatises on which that chapter rests. Second, it gives a single overview of the principal scriptures and treatises cited across the book's twenty chapters. Nothing in this book is invented—every chapter's argument presses down on the original wording of the texts listed below. All quotations have been collated word by word against the CBETA Electronic Tripiṭaka (the Taishō canon and the Manji Zokuzōkyō).

Numbering conventions: **T** refers to the Taishō Shinshū Daizōkyō (volume and text number); **X** to the Manji Shinsan Zokuzōkyō. Where one and the same text is drawn on for different facets in different chapters, the facets are noted separately. One further note: the correspondence of the six gates to the Heart Sūtra (lifted at the volume's close) is the interpretive framework this series undertakes and answers for, not the scripture's explicit text—this declaration stands in the body text of Part Eight, and is not re-flagged entry by entry below.
