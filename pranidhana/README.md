# From the Pāli Vow to Samantabhadra's Conduct
## 《願——從巴利誓願到普賢行願》

**Volume 6 of [The Six Practice Gates](../README.md)** — the final gate, the sealing of the sequence.

*PRAṆIDHĀNA — Aspiration and the Vow, from Pāli Roots to Samantabhadra*

A companion to [*Mindful of the Buddha*](../nian/) (Volume 1), [*The Practice of Giving*](../dana/) (Volume 2), [*Repentance*](../ksama/) (Volume 3), [*From Pāli Vinaya to the Sign-less Precepts*](../sila/) (Volume 4), and [*From the Pāli Jhāna to Silent Illumination and the Huatou*](../samadhi/) (Volume 5). Where the bodhisattva's ultimate nirvana (SAMĀDHI) is the inward consummation, PRAṆIDHĀNA is the vow that turns it outward into the Buddhas' full awakening — the Heart Sūtra's 得阿耨多羅三藐三菩提, the END of the sequence. The vow is not a wish: it is the structural act of turning the whole of the will toward awakening, traced from the Pāli *paṇidhāna* (the bodhisattva's primal vow before Dīpaṃkara) through the four great vows, Amitābha's primal vows, and Samantabhadra's vows of conduct.

With the SAMĀDHI volume sealed, the gate on Part VIII was met and the two closing papers are now released: the three-times Buddhas' attainment of bodhi as the END of the sequence (P19), and the grand reveal — the six-gate ↔ Heart Sūtra mapping (觀／度／空／不／究竟／得菩提 = 念／施／懺／戒／定／願, five movements and one emptiness; repentance the one subtractive gate, the vow alone unsealing 得菩提) — as the series closer (P20). The loop of the whole six-gate sequence closes here, handed back to its sister series (on view and wisdom) and to the three trainings (śīla, samādhi, prajñā).

### Structure

Eight parts, 20 papers (the sealing volume favors concision over expansion):

- **Part I** Pāli Roots — *praṇidhāna* / *samādāna* / *pariṇāmanā* foundations · Sumedha's primal vow before Dīpaṃkara · *adhiṭṭhāna* and the non-regressing vow
- **Part II** The Āgama View — *chanda* (wholesome desire) not *taṇhā* (craving) · the directed and the undirected (*paṇidhi*) in the Nikāyas
- **Part III** Vow as a Perfection — *praṇidhāna* as the eighth *pāramitā* · the ten vows of the ten grounds · Nāgārjuna on vow-power
- **Part IV** The Four Great Vows — the grammar of universal aspiration · the unfinishable vow as a structure, not a target
- **Part V** The Primal Vows of Amitābha — the forty-eight vows as the architecture of a Pure Land · other-power and the grammar of entrusting
- **Part VI** Samantabhadra's Vows of Conduct — vow and conduct are not two · moment-to-moment continuity · transference as the hinge of the tenth vow
- **Part VII** Vow and Emptiness: The Non-Regressing Vow — vowing without a vower · the patience of non-arising · the bodhisattva's refusal of final nirvana
- **Part VIII** The Sealing: Attainment of Bodhi and the Grand Reveal — the three-times Buddhas attain bodhi · what the six gates were all along (the series closer)
