# Concentration, from the Pāli Jhāna to Silent Illumination and the Huatou
## 《定——從巴利禪那到默照看話》

**Volume 5 of [The Six Practice Gates](../README.md)** — 定 SAMĀDHI, the inward gate; sequence position five, between 戒 (restraint) and 願 (aspiration).

*SAMĀDHI — Concentration, from the Pāli Jhāna to Silent Illumination and the Huatou*

A companion to [*Mindful of the Buddha*](../nian/) (Volume 1), [*The Practice of Giving*](../dana/) (Volume 2), [*Repentance*](../ksama/) (Volume 3), and [*From Pāli Vinaya to the Sign-less Precepts*](../sila/) (Volume 4). Where 戒 (SĪLA) sets the boundary of action, 定 gathers awareness inward upon that purified ground — and 願 (Volume 6) opens it back outward as vow.

Of the six gates, 定 correlates with the Heart Sūtra's **究竟** (*ultimate* — 究竟涅槃, the bodhisattva's consummation): the gate that takes the inward turn to its limit. But the volume's through-line is not "the deeper the better." Its spine is the ladder of the four *jhānas* and the shedding of the absorption-factors, read from the Pāli jhāna through the Āgama analysis, the Mahāyāna perfection of *dhyāna*, *prajñā*'s critique of clinging to the meditative state, the *Śūraṅgama*'s great *samādhi*, and the Chan non-duality of concentration-and-wisdom. The Buddha tasted his teachers' formless absolutes and **set them down** — and so the volume's thesis: *the deepest samādhi is one in which even the samādhi-state cannot be grasped; concentration must return to wisdom.*

### Planned Structure

Nine parts, an arc from the Pāli jhāna inward to the limit, then back out to wisdom:

- **Part I** Pāli Roots — *samādhi* / *samatha* / *jhāna*; the Buddha's own concentration, the two tracks, the four-jhāna spine, the object-structure of absorption
- **Part II** The Āgama View — concentration in the Chinese Āgamas
- **Part III** Abhidharma and the Path of Purification — the systematization of concentration (Sarvāstivāda and Theravāda read side by side)
- **Part IV** ★ The Mahāyāna Turn — the perfection of *dhyāna* and the structure of *śamatha-vipaśyanā*
- **Part V** Prajñā and Concentration — Mañjuśrī's sword on the subtle clinging within the meditative state
- **Part VI** The Śūraṅgama — the great *śūraṅgama-samādhi* (the landing-point received from SĪLA)
- **Part VII** ★ Chan — the non-duality of concentration and wisdom, gradual and sudden, silent illumination (默照) and the huatou (看話): the volume's signature, with no counterpart in the earlier volumes
- **Part VIII** The Concentration of the Lotus and the Huayan
- **Part IX** Modern Diagnosis and the Return to Prajñā — concentration is not final; *samādhi* turns back toward the wisdom that releases the grip on the meditative state itself
