# SAMĀDHI: Concentration
### From Pāli Jhāna to Silent Illumination and Huatou

The English edition of **《定——從巴利禪那到默照看話》** by 釋慧鏡 (Shi Huijing)—Volume 5 of the **Six Practice Gates** (六行門).

> Concentration (*samādhi*) is not a tower to climb, nor a button to press; it is a door that gathers inward—not an attainment that deepens the longer you sit, but a subtraction that sets things down rung by rung. And when that door has gathered to its deepest, it goes no further in: it opens back, toward the light of wisdom—and all along, from the Pāli jhāna to silent illumination and huatou, what makes it precious is its willingness to be the sealed chamber in which the lamp of wisdom comes into full use.

This is the accessible *book* layer, rendered into English from the Chinese edition and term-anchored to the bilingual scholarly corpus in this repository (`samadhi/papers/`, P01–P31). It lives here at `samadhi/book/en/`, a part-for-part parallel of the Chinese book under `samadhi/book/`.

## The road

Nine parts, one road:

**Pāli Roots · The Āgama View · Abhidharma and the Path of Purification · The Mahāyāna Turn · Prajñā and Concentration · The Śūraṅgama · Chan · Lotus and Huayan · The Modern Return**

One door, gathered by stages: from the boy's first jhāna beneath the rose-apple tree—and the Buddha's verdict on the deepest concentration of his age, "this dharma does not tend toward nirvāṇa"—through right concentration woven into the eightfold path, the treatise-masters' workshop, the perfection of dhyāna that comes down the mountain, the sword of prajñā that cuts even the clinging to stillness, the Śūraṅgama's "gathering in the mind is the precept," Chan's pacifying of the mind, and the Huayan's single thought that was never apart from the dharma-realm—and back, at the end, to the people of today: a door that gathers to its deepest, then opens back toward the light of wisdom.

## How this edition is made

Each chapter is translated from the approved Chinese chapter and term-anchored to its source paper, so that every doctrinal term uses the rendering the author has already settled. Sūtra and śāstra quotations reuse the verified English from the papers wherever they exist. Romanizations and quick definitions are gathered in [Appendix C · Glossary](appendix/C_glossary.md); each chapter's scriptural sources are indexed in [Appendix A · Academic Notes Index](appendix/A_academic-notes-index.md).
